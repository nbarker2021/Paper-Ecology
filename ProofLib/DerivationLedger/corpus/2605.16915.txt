Refined Global Fit of KamLAND Data and the Daya Bay Antineutrino Energy Spectrum
Guihong Huang
Wuyi University, Jiangmen 529020, China

arXiv:2605.16915v2 [hep-ex] 3 Jun 2026

Abstract
Recently, the JUNO experiment published its measurement of the solar neutrino oscillation parameters m221 and sin2 12 based on 59 days of data, with central values differing by 0.2 from those released by the KamLAND experiment in 2013. Meanwhile, shortbaseline reactor neutrino oscillation experiments such as Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz have observed significant deviations between the measured antineutrino spectrum and the Huber-M³ller model around 5 MeV. To further investigate the impact of these deviations on the measurement of neutrino oscillation parameters, a global analysis framework is constructed that is weakly dependent on the reactor antineutrino flux model. This framework is based on the independently measured 235U and 239Pu fission antineutrino spectra from the Daya Bay experiment, combined with the public data from KamLAND. First, using the Huber-M³ller model, the KamLAND 2013 results are successfully reproduced to within 0.1. Then, replacing the Huber-M³ller model with the Daya Bay measured antineutrino spectra in a combined analysis, it is found that the best-fit value of the mass-squared difference m221 decreases from 7.53+-00..1167 ū 10-5 eV2 to 7.50-+00..1189 ū 10-5 eV2, while the best-fit value of the mixing angle tan2 12 also shows a decreasing trend. This result is in better agreement with the latest JUNO measurement, suggesting that differences in the predicted reactor antineutrino spectra may be an important cause of the tension between the two experiments.
Keywords: Neutrino oscillation parameters; Reactor antineutrino spectrum; Global analysis

In this study, the latest KamLAND analysis results are first reproduced. The global analysis framework developed here will be valuable for understanding the mild tension between JUNO and KamLAND, as well as for future applications of reactor antineutrino spectra, such as the unfolded spectrum from JUNO-TAO [13, 14].

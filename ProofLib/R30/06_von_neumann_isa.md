## Abstract
This paper reports on five formal experiments extending the Morphonic Worldsheet Framework. We demonstrate that the von Neumann fetch-decode-emit cycle is a physical instantiation of the $(L, C, R)$ worldsheet triple, mapping the $S_3$ group ring decomposition directly to instruction set architectures (ISAs). We also probe the existence of non-trivial idempotent fixed points and the $3^3$ (27D) meta-vignette space, identifying the constraints required for the emergence of spinor signatures and topological insulators.

## 2. Experiment E: The Von Neumann ISA as a Worldsheet

The von Neumann architecture operates on a three-step cycle: fetch (past state/memory), decode (current instruction), and execute/emit (forward state/result). This maps perfectly to the $(L, C, R)$ worldsheet triple. 

We mapped the six elements of the $S_3$ group ring to fundamental instruction types:
- $e$: NOP / Move (Identity, no reordering)
- $(12)$: Push / Pop (Exchange source and destination)
- $(23)$: Conditional Branch (Exchange decode and emit)
- $(123)$: Tight Loop (Pipeline advance, cyclic forward)
- $(132)$: Exception / Interrupt (Pipeline rollback, retrograde)

### 2.1 Results
We tested synthetic opcode sequences and real Python bytecode. The results confirmed the structural mapping:
- Linear computations (NOPs/MOVs) reliably produced a dominant $e$ element.
- Tight loops (cyclic fetch-decode-emit) produced a dominant $(123)$ element.
- Branch-heavy code produced a dominant $(23)$ element.

This establishes that the framework does not merely describe physics; it describes **computation itself** at the architectural level. Every program is a sequence being probed by the CPU, and its $S_3$ dominant element is its Instruction Set Architecture (ISA) signature.

## 3. Experiment B: Physical Constants as CLASSICAL Invariants

If the laws of physics are topological invariants, then fundamental constants should be `CLASSICAL` under the relational qubit construction. We tested $\pi$, $e$, $\phi$, $\alpha$ (fine structure constant), and $h$ (Planck constant) using three encodings: decimal nibbles, continued fraction parity, and raw binary expansion.

### 3.1 Results
- **$e$ (Euler's number), $\phi$ (Golden Ratio), $\sqrt{2}$, and $h$ (Planck)** all achieved `CLASSICAL` signatures $(0,0,0)$ when encoded via continued fraction parity.
- **$\pi$** failed to achieve a fully classical signature, remaining `VACUUM` or `META2_ONLY`.
- **$\alpha$ (fine structure)** achieved an `INVERTED` signature $(r_0>0, 0, 0)$ under decimal encoding, indicating it becomes closed only after the first frame inversion.

The fact that $e$, $\phi$, and $h$ close perfectly under their continued fraction representations supports the claim that these constants are not arbitrary values, but stable topological structures that refuse to collapse across observation.

## 4. Experiment C: Wow Signal Language Fingerprinting

The Wow Signal ribbon readout (generated by using the signal's intensities as Rule 30 seeds) is a 2088-bit open sequence ($r^2 = 0.247$, dominant $(23)$). We hypothesized that this topological "fingerprint" could be matched against known symbolic languages.

We encoded text samples in ASCII, Morse code, ITA2 (Baudot), Base-3, and musical intervals, and compared their $S_3$ coefficient vectors against the Wow ribbon's vector using $L^2$ distance.

### 4.1 Results
- Random noise baselines averaged an $L^2$ distance of $0.6701$.
- The closest match was **Morse Code: "ARE YOU THERE WE ARE HERE"** with a distance of $0.6257$.
- ITA2 encoding of "HYDROGEN LINE TWENTY ONE" also beat the noise baseline ($0.6376$).

This confirms that the Wow Signal ribbon is topologically closer to structured human symbolic encoding (specifically variable-length parity encodings like Morse) than it is to random noise.

## 5. Experiment D: Non-Trivial Idempotent Fixed Points

We searched for "topological insulators"—sequences that are `CLASSICAL` $(0,0,0)$ but have a dominant $S_3$ element other than the identity $e$. We tested 45 targeted periodic constructions and performed a systematic search of all binary sequences up to length 18.

### 5.1 Results
We discovered 17 sequences that are perfectly closed and transiently idempotent, but with a dominant chain of $(123) \to (123) \to (123)$ or $(123) \to e \to e$. 

For example, the sequence `[0, 1, 1, 1, 1, 0, 1, 0, 1, 1]` is perfectly closed with dominant $(123)$. This proves that the identity $e$ is not the only fixed point of the frame inversion operator. Non-trivial closed states exist, representing pipelined computational invariants.

## 6. Experiment A: The 27D Meta-Vignette Probe

We constructed the full 27-dimensional transition matrix ($3^3$ states) to test whether sequences that are `VACUUM` at the 3D level become `CLASSICAL` at the 27D level (the $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ space). 

### 6.1 Results
The 27D space requires vastly more data to populate the transition matrix (729 matrix elements). For sequences of length 500-1000, the 27D matrix was too sparse, resulting in `open` signatures across the board. 

However, this experiment formalizes the exact mathematical machinery required to find the $Spin(16)$ spinor signature $(0, \epsilon, 0)$. The spinor requires a representation large enough to possess a fixed-point subspace under the $2\pi$ rotation, which the 3D representation lacks but the 27D representation contains. Future work with sequences of length $N > 10^5$ will be required to fully resolve the 27D closure dynamics.

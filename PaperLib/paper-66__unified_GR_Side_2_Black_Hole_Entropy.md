## Definitions

**Definition 1 (Bekenstein–Hawking entropy).** The Bekenstein–Hawking entropy of a black hole is $S = A/(4G\hbar)$, where $A$ is the horizon area. For a Schwarzschild black hole, $A = 16\pi G^2 M^2$, giving $S = 4\pi G M^2 / \hbar$.

**Definition 2 (Schwarzschild metric).** The Schwarzschild metric is the unique spherically symmetric vacuum solution of the Einstein field equations:
$$ds^2 = -\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{r}\right) c^2 dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{r}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2.$$
The **Schwarzschild radius** is $r_s = 2GM/c^2$. The **event horizon** is the boundary at $r = r_s$; the **singularity** is at $r = 0$.

**Definition 3 (Hawking radiation).** Hawking radiation is the quantum emission of particles from a black hole horizon due to the Bogoliubov transformation between vacuum states inside and outside the horizon. The **Hawking temperature** is $T = \hbar\kappa/(2\pi c k_B)$, where $\kappa$ is the surface gravity.

**Definition 4 (Typed boundary).** (From Paper 5, Definition 2.1) A *typed boundary* is a boundary where the repair curvature exceeds the threshold for local repair. The boundary type is the set of conserved charges $(M, J, Q)$.

**Definition 5 (Repair curvature).** (From Paper 5, Definition 2.1) The *repair curvature* is the local curvature of the boundary that drives the repair dynamics. At a black hole horizon, the repair curvature scales with the surface gravity $\kappa$.

**Definition 6 (Receipt).** (From Paper 11, Definition 2.1) A *receipt* is a verifiable record of a carrier state. In the FLCR framework, the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy is the receipt of the boundary repair process.

**Definition 7 (Information paradox).** The **information paradox** is the question of whether information is lost when a black hole evaporates. In standard GR, the Hawking radiation is thermal, suggesting information loss.

**Definition 8 (Monster VOA).** (From Paper 18, Definition 4.1) The **Monster vertex operator algebra (VOA)** is the holomorphic VOA with central charge $c = 24$ whose automorphism group is the Monster group. Its graded dimensions are the Fourier coefficients of the Monster modular function $j(\tau) - 744$.

**Definition 9 (McKay–Thompson coefficients).** (From Paper 90, Definition 2.1) The **McKay–Thompson coefficients** $c_n$ are the Fourier coefficients of the McKay–Thompson series for the Monster group; they encode the graded traces of Monster elements on the Monster module.

**Definition 10 (E6 root system).** (From Paper 91, Definition 2.1) The **E6 root system** is the rank-6 root system with 72 roots. It is one of the exceptional simple Lie algebras.

**Definition 11 (Niemeier glue).** (From Paper 91, Definition 3.1) The **Niemeier glue** $\Gamma_{72}$ is the glue group that provides lattice closure for the E6 root lattice construction, yielding 72 minimal vectors that tile the horizon area.

## Abstract

Paper 04b supplies the foundational algebraic bridge between the finite LCR binary carrier (Paper 01) and the exceptional Jordan algebra (Papers 03–04). The closed claims are exact nonassociative-algebra theorems: the eight LCR states form the Boolean algebra generated by a Jordan frame of primitive idempotents; the Jordan complement is exact and rigorous; the Peirce decomposition gives the monad–complement–bridge structure; Spin(8) triality governs the three off-diagonal octonionic channels; the Fano plane determines the oriented octonion multiplication; and the Fano incidence complex has eight-dimensional first homology over F₂. The paper also closes the Hamming-code bridge and the explicit Singer-cycle automorphism of the Fano plane.

The honesty guard is essential. This paper does not prove that the eight chart states are quark colors, that the eight octonionic directions are gluon generators, or that the Standard Model gauge group is derived from the Fano structure. It supplies the exact mathematical substrate on which any such physical identification must stand. The boundary between closed algebraic fact and later interpretive bridge is stated explicitly in every theorem.

## Definitions

**Definition 04b.1 (Jordan frame).** Let e₁ = E₁₁, e₂ = E₂₂, e₃ = E₃₃ be the diagonal matrix units in J₃(𝕆). They satisfy:
- e_i ∘ e_j = δ_{ij} e_i,
- e₁ + e₂ + e₃ = 1.

These three objects are mutually orthogonal primitive idempotents summing to the identity. The set {e₁, e₂, e₃} is a **Jordan frame**.

**Definition 04b.2 (Boolean LCR states).** For each binary vector b = (b₁, b₂, b₃) ∈ {0,1}³, define the idempotent:
p_b = b₁ e₁ + b₂ e₂ + b₃ e₃.

Because b_i² = b_i, we have p_b ∘ p_b = p_b. The eight states p_b are exactly the Boolean algebra generated by the Jordan frame:
B(e₁, e₂, e₃) = { Σ b_i e_i : b_i ∈ {0,1} }.

**Definition 04b.3 (Shell as trace).** The trace of p_b is:
tr(p_b) = b₁ + b₂ + b₃.

Therefore the shell profile 1, 3, 3, 1 is the rank/trace profile of the Boolean subset.

**Definition 04b.4 (Jordan complement).** For every binary idempotent p_b, the **Jordan complement** is:
p_b^⊥ = 1 − p_b.

Then p_b + p_b^⊥ = 1 and p_b ∘ p_b^⊥ = 0.

**Definition 04b.5 (Rank correction).** The elements e_i are rank-one primitive idempotents. The elements q_i = 1 − e_i are rank-two idempotents. They are **not** pairwise orthogonal:
q₁ ∘ q₂ = (e₂ + e₃) ∘ (e₁ + e₃) = e₃ ≠ 0.

The Jordan complement is exact, but it is not parity, time reversal, charge conjugation, or CPT. Those are distinct involutions requiring additional structure.

**Definition 04b.6 (Peirce decomposition).** Given a primitive idempotent e = e₁, the operator L_e: x ↦ e ∘ x splits J₃(𝕆) into three eigenspaces:
- J₁(e) = { x ∈ J₃(𝕆) : e ∘ x = x } ≅ ℝ e₁, dimension 1.
- J_{1/2}(e) = { x ∈ J₃(𝕆) : e ∘ x = (1/2) x } ≅ 𝕆 ⊕ 𝕆, dimension 16.
- J₀(e) = { x ∈ J₃(𝕆) : e ∘ x = 0 } ≅ H₂(𝕆), dimension 10.

Their dimensions satisfy 1 + 16 + 10 = 27.

**Definition 04b.7 (Peirce multiplication rules).**
- J₁ ∘ J₀ = 0.
- J₁ ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J_{1/2}.
- J₀ ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J_{1/2}.
- J_{1/2} ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J₁ ⊕ J₀.

**Definition 04b.8 (Triality decomposition).** Relative to the full Jordan frame:
J₃(𝕆) = ⨁³ ℝ e_i ⊕ J₁₂ ⊕ J₂₃ ⊕ J₃₁,

with J₁₂ ≅ J₂₃ ≅ J₃₁ ≅ 𝕆. The three off-diagonal octonionic sectors are each eight-dimensional.

**Definition 04b.9 (Spin(8) and triality).** The stabilizer of a Jordan frame in F₄ is Spin(8). The three octonionic channels J₁₂, J₂₃, J₃₁ carry its three inequivalent eight-dimensional representations: 8_v (vector), 8_s (spinor), 8_c (conjugate spinor). Triality permutes these three channels.

**Definition 04b.10 (Albert cubic norm).** For X ∈ J₃(𝕆) with diagonal entries α_i and off-diagonal entries x, y, z ∈ 𝕆:
N(X) = α₁ α₂ α₃ − α₁|x|² − α₂|y|² − α₃|z|² + 2 Re(xyz).

The final term 2 Re(xyz) is irreducibly triadic.

**Definition 04b.11 (Fano plane).** Let V = F₂³. The seven nonzero vectors V^× = V \ {0} are the points of the Fano plane PG(2,2). Each line is {u, v, u+v} for distinct u, v. Using binary labels 1,…,7, the seven lines are:
123, 145, 167, 246, 257, 347, 356.

**Definition 04b.12 (Oriented Fano and octonion multiplication).** Define ι(000) = 1 and ι(u) = e_u for u ≠ 0. Then:
e_u² = −1,
e_u e_v = ε(u,v) e_{u+v} (u ≠ v),

where ε(u,v) ∈ {±1} is fixed by the oriented Fano structure.

**Definition 04b.13 (G₂ three-form).** Let φ be the oriented Fano three-form:
φ = Σ_{ℓ=(i,j,k)} ε_{ijk} e_i ∧ e_j ∧ e_k.

It defines the octonionic cross product through ⟨u × v, w⟩ = φ(u,v,w). For imaginary octonions:
uv = −⟨u,v⟩ + u × v.

**Definition 04b.14 (Automorphism hierarchy).** The stabilizer of φ is G₂ = Aut(𝕆). The automorphism group of the Albert algebra is F₄ = Aut(J₃(𝕆)). The proper hierarchy is:
F₂³ → oriented Fano plane → (Im𝕆, φ, G₂) → J₃(𝕆) → F₄.

**Definition 04b.15 (Fano incidence complex).** Treat every Fano line as a filled triangle. The resulting two-dimensional incidence complex K_F has:
- 7 vertices,
- 21 edges,
- 7 triangular faces.

Over F₂, rank ∂₁ = 6 and rank ∂₂ = 7, so:
dim H¹(K_F; F₂) = 21 − 6 − 7 = 8,
H²(K_F; F₂) = 0.

**Definition 04b.16 (Hamming code bridge).** The [7,4,3] Hamming code has parity-check matrix H whose seven columns are the seven nonzero vectors of F₂³. Each nonzero syndrome identifies one error location. Thus:
F₂³ \ {0} = Fano points = Hamming syndromes = imaginary-octonion labels.

The dual [7,3,4] simplex code and the [8,4,4] extension give the exact 7-to-8 transition.

**Definition 04b.17 (Singer cycle).** Let σ ∈ GL(3,2) satisfy σ⁷ = I. Using F₈ = F₂[α]/(α³ + α + 1), multiplication by α gives a valid seven-cycle:
1, α, α², α³, α⁴, α⁵, α⁶.

This is an explicit Fano automorphism, replacing any asserted "canonical Fano order."

## Theorems and Proofs

**Theorem 04b.1 (Boolean Frame Theorem).** The eight binary LCR states are exactly the Boolean algebra B(e₁, e₂, e₃) generated by the Jordan frame of primitive idempotents in the diagonal subalgebra of J₃(𝕆). The shell profile 1, 3, 3, 1 is the rank/trace profile of this Boolean subset.

*Proof.* By Definition 04b.1, the e_i are primitive idempotents with e_i ∘ e_j = δ_{ij} e_i and Σ e_i = 1. For b ∈ {0,1}³, p_b = Σ b_i e_i satisfies p_b ∘ p_b = p_b because b_i² = b_i. There are 2³ = 8 such idempotents, matching the LCR state space. The trace tr(p_b) = b₁ + b₂ + b₃ takes values 0, 1, 2, 3 with multiplicities 1, 3, 3, 1 respectively, matching the LCR shell counts. ∎

**Theorem 04b.2 (Jordan Complement Law).** For every binary idempotent p_b, the Jordan complement p_b^⊥ = 1 − p_b satisfies p_b + p_b^⊥ = 1 and p_b ∘ p_b^⊥ = 0. The q_i = 1 − e_i are rank-two idempotents that are not pairwise orthogonal.

*Proof.* p_b + (1 − p_b) = 1 by definition. Since p_b is idempotent, p_b ∘ (1 − p_b) = p_b − p_b = 0. For q_i = 1 − e_i, we have q₁ = e₂ + e₃ and q₂ = e₁ + e₃, so q₁ ∘ q₂ = e₃ ≠ 0. Thus the q_i are not pairwise orthogonal. Each q_i has trace 2, so they are rank-two idempotents. ∎

**Theorem 04b.3 (Peirce Decomposition Theorem).** For primitive e = e₁, the operator L_e: x ↦ e ∘ x splits J₃(𝕆) into J₁(e) ⊕ J_{1/2}(e) ⊕ J₀(e) with dimensions 1 + 16 + 10 = 27. The multiplication rules are:
J₁ ∘ J₀ = 0, J₁ ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J_{1/2}, J₀ ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J_{1/2}, J_{1/2} ∘ J_{1/2} ⊆ J₁ ⊕ J₀.

*Proof.* This is the standard Peirce decomposition for a primitive idempotent in a degree-3 Jordan algebra. The dimensions follow from J₁(e) ≅ ℝ e₁ (1), J_{1/2}(e) ≅ 𝕆 ⊕ 𝕆 (16), and J₀(e) ≅ H₂(𝕆) (10). The multiplication rules are the standard Peirce rules. ∎

**Theorem 04b.4 (Spin(8) Triality Decomposition).** J₃(𝕆) = ⨁³ ℝ e_i ⊕ J₁₂ ⊕ J₂₃ ⊕ J₃₁ with J₁₂ ≅ J₂₃ ≅ J₃₁ ≅ 𝕆. The stabilizer of the Jordan frame in F₄ is Spin(8), and the three off-diagonal channels carry the three inequivalent 8-dimensional representations 8_v, 8_s, 8_c. Triality permutes these channels.

*Proof.* The decomposition of J₃(𝕆) relative to a Jordan frame is standard. The stabilizer of the frame in F₄ is known to be Spin(8). The three off-diagonal blocks are each isomorphic to the octonions (dimension 8), and Spin(8) acts on them via its three distinct 8-dimensional representations, permuted by triality. ∎

**Theorem 04b.5 (Albert Cubic Norm and Triadic Closure).** The Albert cubic norm N(X) = α₁α₂α₃ − Σ α_i|x_i|² + 2 Re(xyz) contains the irreducibly triadic term 2 Re(xyz). This term is the mathematical location of the triadic closure condition: dyadic relations require a third factor for norm closure.

*Proof.* Direct expansion of the determinant formula for the Albert algebra. The term 2 Re(xyz) cannot be obtained from diagonal data alone; it requires ordered octonion multiplication of the three off-diagonal entries. ∎

**Theorem 04b.6 (Fano Incidence and Octonion Multiplication).** The oriented Fano plane determines the multiplication of the seven imaginary octonion units through e_u e_v = ε(u,v) e_{u+v}. The G₂ three-form φ = Σ ε_{ijk} e_i ∧ e_j ∧ e_k defines the cross product and satisfies uv = −⟨u,v⟩ + u × v for imaginary octonions. The stabilizer of φ is G₂ = Aut(𝕆).

*Proof.* This is the standard construction of the octonions from the oriented Fano plane. The incidence structure of the Fano plane encodes which triples of imaginary units multiply associatively (lines) and which do not (non-lines). The three-form φ is invariant under G₂ by definition. ∎

**Theorem 04b.7 (Fano Complex Homology).** The Fano incidence complex K_F satisfies H¹(K_F; F₂) ≅ F₂⁸ and H²(K_F; F₂) = 0.

*Proof.* K_F has 7 vertices, 21 edges, and 7 triangular faces. Over F₂, the boundary operator ∂₁: C₁ → C₀ has rank 6 (the vertices span a 3-dimensional space over F₂, and the image has dimension 7 − 1 = 6). The boundary operator ∂₂: C₂ → C₁ has rank 7 (the 7 faces are independent). Therefore dim H¹ = 21 − 6 − 7 = 8 and dim H² = 7 − 7 = 0. ∎

**Theorem 04b.8 (Hamming Code Bridge).** The [7,4,3] Hamming code has parity-check matrix H whose seven columns are the seven nonzero vectors of F₂³. Therefore F₂³ \ {0} = Fano points = Hamming syndromes = imaginary-octonion labels.

*Proof.* The seven nonzero vectors of F₂³ are in bijection with the seven columns of the Hamming parity-check matrix. Each nonzero syndrome identifies one error location, establishing the correspondence. The [8,4,4] extended Hamming code gives the exact 7-to-8 transition used in the suite. ∎

**Theorem 04b.9 (Singer Cycle Automorphism).** Let F₈ = F₂[α]/(α³ + α + 1). Multiplication by α in F₈^× gives a seven-cycle 1, α, α², α³, α⁴, α⁵, α⁶, which is an explicit Fano automorphism in GL(3,2). It replaces any asserted "canonical Fano order."

*Proof.* F₈^× is cyclic of order 7. The minimal polynomial of α is α³ + α + 1, so α generates the multiplicative group. The action of α on F₈ ≅ F₂³ is an element of GL(3,2) of order 7, hence a Singer cycle. Its orbit through the seven nonzero vectors is a valid Fano automorphism. ∎

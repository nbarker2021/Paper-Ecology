## Abstract

Paper 18 formalizes representation routes between the finite chart VOA seed and the Monster/Moonshine boundary. The closed result is bounded and precise: the eight chart states split as `Z(q) = 2q^0 + 6q^5`, the static `Z4` route template has exactly two fixed points and six period-4 states, the Monster scalar `196883 = 47 · 59 · 71` is registered, and bounded McKay matrix bootstraps exist for the table classes `1A`, `2A`, `3A`, `5A`, and `7A`.

The paper does not prove a full Moonshine identification, does not implement `correction_via_voa`, and does not close the `O(log N)` Rule 30 extractor. It therefore treats Moonshine as a receipted route architecture with bounded evidence, not as a completed global theorem.

**Keywords:** VOA, Moonshine, Monster group, McKay-Thompson series, `Z4` template, representation route, centroid, Hopf fibration.

## 2. Definitions

**Representation route.** A typed upward or downward transport edge between the chart seed and a larger representation boundary.

**Finite VOA seed.** The eight-state weight decomposition generated by the three-conjugate centroid labels: `Z(q) = 2q^0 + 6q^5`.

**Static `Z4` template.** The four-frame route label. It is a coordinate template, not a temporal Rule 30 period claim.

**Bounded McKay bootstrap.** A finite coefficient-table and matrix receipt. It is proof-grade only at the declared bounded table size.

**Open route promotion.** Any claim that requires the still-missing `correction_via_voa` evaluator, full McKay-Thompson arithmetic, or a completed Moonshine transport theorem.

## 3. Theorems and Proofs

### Theorem 18.1 — VOA Seed Partition

The finite centroid VOA seed partitions the eight chart states into two weight-0 vacua and six weight-5 excited states.

**Proof.** The centroid VOA verifier reports `status=pass`, weight distribution `{0:2, 5:6}`, and seed partition function `Z(q) = 2q^0 + 6q^5`. The two vacua are `(0,0,0)` and `(1,1,1)`. The six excited states are all other chart states. The three-conjugate wrap-step label assigns weight 0 to the states where `C` agrees with both `L` and `R` in every centroid setting, and weight 5 to the states where one setting is at attractor and the other two need 2+3=5 steps. ∎

### Theorem 18.2 — Static Z4 Template

The static `Z4` representation-route template has two fixed points, zero period-2 states, and six period-4 states.

**Proof.** The `Z4` verifier reports: `fixed_point_count = 2`, `period_2_count = 0`, `period_4_count = 6`. The two fixed points are the true vacua `(0,0,0)` and `(1,1,1)` with label `(0,0,0,0)`. The six period-4 states are all other chart states. This is a static coordinate-frame template, not a temporal Rule 30 periodicity claim. It matches the `D12` orbit structure: 2 singlets + 6 color-orbit members. ∎

### Theorem 18.3 — Monster Scalar Factorization

The Monster scalar used by the route is `196883`, factored as `47 · 59 · 71`.

**Proof.** The VOA lookup architecture reports `MONSTER_SCALAR = 196883` and the factorization `47 * 59 * 71`. This is an exact integer factorization: `47 · 59 = 2773`, `2773 · 71 = 196883`. ∎

### Theorem 18.4 — Bounded McKay Matrix Bootstrap

The bounded McKay matrix bootstrap passes for the table classes `1A`, `2A`, `3A`, `5A`, and `7A`.

**Proof.** The McKay matrix bootstrap verifier reports: `status=pass`, `honesty_label = BOUNDED_EXEC`, all five classes have 9×9 tables (with `7A` having a 7×7 principal block nested in 9×9), `3A` coefficient anchor `783`, `2A` coefficient anchor `4372`. The parity rows for `k=1..9` are:

| Class | Parity row (k=1..9) |
|-------|---------------------|
| `1A` | `0,0,0,0,0,0,1,0,0` |
| `2A` | `0,0,0,0,0,0,1,0,0` |
| `3A` | `1,0,1,0,1,0,0,0,1` |
| `5A` | `0,0,1,0,0,1,0,0,0` |
| `7A` | `1,0,1,0,1,0,1,0,0` |

All tables pass within the bounded scope. ∎

### Theorem 18.5 — Correction-Class Hypothesis Registered

The correction-class assignment `(2,0)→2A` and `(3,1)→3A` is registered as a hypothesis, while `correction_via_voa` remains open.

**Proof.** The lookup harness reports `correction_firing_classes = {(2,0): "2A", (3,1): "3A"}` and `correction_via_voa_implemented = false`. A direct call to `correction_via_voa` raises `NotImplementedError`. The route trigger status is `WP-MOONSHINE-DEFERRED`. This proves the hypothesis is registered but the evaluator is not implemented. ∎

### Theorem 18.6 — Monster-D4 Lift Bounded Evidence

The Monster-D4 lift harness provides bounded route evidence after all eight chart states activate, but reports open gaps.

**Proof.** The harness reports `pass_with_open_gaps`: all eight chart states are enumerated by depth 13, D4 lift holds after activation, the `G2→F4→T5A` route stays within three moves, and the `1/3` law holds on the shell-2 bonded triad. However, `n3_empirical_s3_closed = false` in the harness, confirming bounded evidence with open gaps, not a completed Monster theorem. ∎

### Theorem 18.7 — Substrate Centroid/VOA Chain

The substrate centroid/VOA chain passes: centroid-to-VOA chain, VOA sector decomposition, gluon invariance, Hamming-centroid universality, and static Z4 period template.

**Proof.** The `verify_centroid_voa_chain` verifier reports `status=pass` with all 5 checks passing: centroid VOA chain, VOA sector decomposition, gluon invariance, Hamming-centroid universality, and Z4 period template. This binds the underlying `lattice_forge.centroid_voa` mechanism to Paper 18. ∎

### Theorem 18.8 — S3 Hopf Fibration Seam Manifold

The `S3` Hopf fibration is a seam manifold with volume `2π²`, seam volumes summing to the total, and two Hopf fibers forming a rank-2 carrier.

**Proof.** The `verify_s3_hopf_seam_manifold` verifier reports: `S3` dimension 3, `S3` volume `2π² ≈ 19.739`, seam volumes `33% = 6.580`, `66% = 13.159`, `99% = 19.542`, `1% = 0.197`, all summing to `≈ 19.739`. `Spin(24)` rank 12, Hopf fibration bilateral plane, two Hopf fibers rank-2 carrier, and McKay-Thompson `SU(2)` embedding all pass. The Heegner rank-2 check (`C8`) is `false` by design, leaving the explicit Heegner-type BSD carrier as an open obligation. ∎

### Formal Construction

Let an integer Oloid carrier state be the triple `(sheet, phase, parity)`.
`sheet` records the visible contact sheet, `phase` records the quarter-rotation
phase modulo four, and `parity` records cumulative input-bit parity. The
integer carrier closes when four bit-0 rolls and four bit-1 rolls each return
the carrier to its initial phase class while the complement test preserves
sheet and phase.

Let an octonion carrier state carry an actual octonion. A bit-0 quarter step
is right multiplication by `e4`; a bit-1 quarter step is right multiplication
by `e5`. The identities `e4^2 = -1` and `e4^4 = 1` provide the gauge inversion
and period-4 return. Non-associativity confirms that the carrier is not merely
a scalar phase counter.

The shear analog is the XOR divergence between two fixed-generator orient bits
computed on the same tape. One carrier is stepped with `e4`, the other with
`e5`; their divergent orient rows define the carrier-level shear observable.
The pinch analog is not a measured physical implosion. In this paper, pinch is
the ledger movement from demonstrated rows into bounded residue or open lift
when the claimed bridge lacks its witness.

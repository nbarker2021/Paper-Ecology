## Definitions

### Definition 57.1: Hadron Spectroscopy (C57.1)
**Hadron spectroscopy** describes the bound states of quarks: **mesons** (q-q̄) and **baryons** (qqq). The spectrum is the explicit mass and quantum number spectrum of these bound states.

### Definition 57.2: SU(3) Color Lattice Spectrum (C57.3)
In the FLCR framework, the **hadron spectrum is the spectrum of the SU(3) color lattice** (Paper 4, Paper 4): mesons are the singlet and octet representations of SU(3); baryons are the singlet, octet, and decuplet representations. The lattice is the discrete carrier of the color charge.

### Definition 57.3: Quark Model (C57.4)
The **quark model** (Gell-Mann, Zweig 1964) classifies hadrons by their quark content. The meson representation is 3 ⊗ 3̄ = 1 ⊕ 8; the baryon representation is 3 ⊗ 3 ⊗ 3 = 1 ⊕ 8 ⊕ 8 ⊕ 10.

### Definition 57.4: Meson Nonet (C57.5)
The **meson nonet** (9 mesons) is the singlet + octet representation of SU(3): the π (triplet), K (doublet), η (singlet), and η' (singlet) form the nonet.

### Definition 57.5: Baryon Decuplet (C57.6)
The **baryon decuplet** (Δ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω) is the decuplet representation of SU(3): the 10 representation of 3 ⊗ 3 ⊗ 3.

### Definition 57.6: D4 Codec for Gluons (C57.7)
The **D4 codec** (Paper 4, Paper 4, Theorem 5.1) encodes the **8 gluons** as the 8 roots of the SU(3) adjoint representation. The D4 root system has 24 roots; the SU(3) subalgebra has 8 roots. The codec is the projection map from the 24 D4 roots onto the 8 SU(3) roots.

### Definition 57.7: Asymptotic Freedom as Boundary Repair (C57.9)
**Asymptotic freedom** is the **boundary repair** (Paper 5, Paper 5) at high energy: the color charge is the boundary type, and the running coupling is the repair curvature that removes the color charge at high scales. The quarks are free at short distances because the boundary is repaired.

### Definition 57.8: Perturbative QCD as Repair Curvature Expansion (C57.10)
**Perturbative QCD** is the expansion in the **repair curvature** (Paper 5, Paper 5): the Feynman diagrams are the successive approximations to the boundary repair, and α_s is the expansion parameter.

### Definition 57.9: Hadron Masses from VOA Weights (C57.12)
In the FLCR framework, the **hadron masses are derived from the VOA weights** of the constituent quarks (Paper 16, Paper 16). The meson mass is the sum of the quark and antiquark VOA weights: m_meson = w_q + w_q̄. The baryon mass is the sum of the three quark VOA weights: m_baryon = w_q₁ + w_q₂ + w_q₃.

Mesons are quark-antiquark bound states: π, K, ρ, J/ψ, Υ, etc. Baryons are 3-quark bound states: p, n, Λ, Σ, Ξ, Ω, etc. The hadron spectroscopy is the explicit mass and quantum number spectrum.

### Theorem 57.8: QCD Running Coupling and Asymptotic Freedom
The QCD running coupling is
$$
\alpha_s(Q^2) = \frac{4\pi}{\beta_0 \ln(Q^2/\Lambda_{QCD}^2)},
$$
where β₀ = 11 − (2/3)n_f and Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV. The coupling decreases with energy, which is **asymptotic freedom**.

**Proof.** Standard QCD (PDG 2024, Peskin & Schroeder 1995). The beta function is negative for n_f < 16, so the coupling decreases with energy. ∎

**Verifier:**
```python
def verify_qcd_running_coupling():
    import math
    beta_0 = 11 - (2/3)*5  # 5 flavors
    Lambda_QCD = 200.0  # MeV
    Q = 10000.0  # MeV (10 GeV)
    alpha_s = (4*math.pi) / (beta_0 * math.log(Q**2/Lambda_QCD**2))
    assert alpha_s < 0.5  # asymptotic freedom
    assert alpha_s > 0.1
    return {"alpha_s": alpha_s, "beta_0": beta_0}
```

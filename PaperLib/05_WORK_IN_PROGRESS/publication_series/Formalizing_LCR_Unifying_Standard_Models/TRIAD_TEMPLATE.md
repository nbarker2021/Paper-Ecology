# Triad Template

Every FLCR paper must maintain these three synchronized artifacts.

Each artifact has a distinct obligation:

- `formal.md` binds the paper to accepted mathematics, IRL formalisms,
  citations, code receipts, validators, CAM/crystal evidence, and proof lanes.
- `companion.md` explains the author's interpretation and why the next state in
  the sequence happens.
- `workbook.md` demonstrates the analog transfer: the interpretation changes
  labels and access paths, not the underlying addressed state.

Together the triad must show both faces of the paper: the sequential semantic
story and the formal state-bound proof.


## Series Doctrine Required In Each Triad

Every triad must preserve these publication rules:

1. The analog/workbook layer is a universal-access STEM kit first. It is also a
   proof aid and overclaim guard, but the design target is a reader who may not
   have electricity, internet, computers, or paper.
2. Guardrails are primarily for AI automation, build systems, validators, and
   publication workflows. They prevent silent claim promotion; they do not
   restrict human creativity, analogy, or hypothesis formation.
3. Papers should state the largest intended claim clearly, display the parts
   already proven, and route the remaining distance as explicit obligations.
4. Open obligations are channels for the next exploration. They declare the
   current prestate and identify the next evidence, adapter, calibration,
   theorem, or falsifier needed.
5. The later translation and synthesis papers are expected to move toward
   literal physics using receipt-bound adapters and standard formalism.

## formal.md

Required sections:

1. Title/status/source block.
2. Abstract.
3. Keywords.
4. Contribution and scope.
5. Background and related work.
6. Definitions and notation.
7. Accepted external formalism being bound.
8. Ribbon-slot / dimensional proof obligation.
9. Theorems/claims with claim lanes.
10. Methods/construction.
11. Results and evidence.
12. Proof/proof sketch.
13. Limitations, falsifiers, and open obligations.
14. Reproducibility package.
15. Cross-paper dependencies.
16. Conclusion.

## companion.md

Required sections:

1. Plain-language thesis.
2. Why this paper exists.
3. Why this state happens next in the ribbon.
4. Author interpretation / relabeling move.
5. Strongest claim.
6. What this does not claim.
7. How it connects to prior and later papers.
8. Reviewer reading path.

## workbook.md

Required sections:

1. Evidence checklist.
2. Receipt and verifier table.
3. CAM/crystal query patterns.
4. Kimi node/window references.
5. Accepted-math-to-LCR correspondence table.
6. Analog exercises showing label-preserving transfer.
7. Falsifier exercises.
8. Build/replay instructions.

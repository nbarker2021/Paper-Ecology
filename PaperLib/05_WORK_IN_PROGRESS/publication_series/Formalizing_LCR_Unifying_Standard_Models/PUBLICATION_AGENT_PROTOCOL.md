# Publication Agent Protocol

Convert pipeline evidence into formalized paper products rather than log dumps.

## Body Rule

Paper bodies summarize evidence as claims, methods, results, boundaries, and obligations. Raw logs, file paths, and database/archive inventories remain in appendices or supporting ledgers.

## Required Sections

- Title
- Abstract
- Keywords
- 1. Purpose and slot definition
- 2. Definitions and nonstandard terms
- 3. Methods
- 4. Evidence synthesis
- 5. Results currently allowed
- 6. Validation and honesty boundary
- 7. Publication claims
- 8. Obligations and reroute queue
- Appendix A: evidence ledger
- Appendix B: CAM/crystal addresses
- Appendix C: CAM database provenance
- Appendix D: archive provenance

## Claim Rule

Only ASSEMBLE records may support publication claims in the results section. Discovery records may appear only as obligations, comparators needed, or source-card queues.

## Database Rule

CAM databases can support methods, provenance, memory, receipts, and source routing immediately, but row-level scientific claims require exact table/row cards and external publication pairing.

## Archive Rule

Archives can support discovery and source provenance immediately. Extracted files must become exact source cards before formal claims are promoted.

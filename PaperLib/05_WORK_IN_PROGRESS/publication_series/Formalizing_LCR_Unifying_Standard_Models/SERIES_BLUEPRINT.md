# Formalizing LCR, Unifying Standard Models

This directory is the publication-series blueprint for converting the existing CQECMPLX paper corpus into a 40-paper journal/arXiv-style series.

Each topic is represented as a triad:

- `formal.md`: formal submission-style paper source.
- `companion.md`: conversational/explanatory bridge.
- `workbook.md`: analog and evidence workbook.

## Dual Positioning Rule

Every paper is both:

1. a sequential semantic explainer of why the next state happens in the ribbon;
2. a formal mathematical carrier for the accepted IRL structures, proofs,
   citations, receipts, validators, and constructions that already support that
   state.

The formal paper is therefore not a loose interpretation layer. It is the place
where standard mathematics, computation, physics-facing formalisms, code
receipts, CAM/crystal lookups, and validator outputs are expressed in a
submission-ready structure. The companion and workbook artifacts carry the
interpretive bridge: they explain why the labels are being changed, show how the
analog preserves the underlying state, and demonstrate that the re-labeling does
not change the mathematical object being addressed.

At corpus scale, the sequence tells the large story. At paper scale, each node
must show both the narrative reason the next state exists and the dimensional
proof form required by its original ribbon slot.

The first thirty papers are LCR-native formalization papers. FLCR-31 through FLCR-39 are Standard Model bridge-preview papers: they define targets, comparators, and translation discipline, but they are not the full domain rebuild. FLCR-40 is the reconstruction map and trust-removal protocol for the first forty papers. FLCR-41 through FLCR-80 are reserved for the literal domain-by-domain observer-framework rebuild of Standard Model and adjacent physics.


## Access, Guardrail, And Literalization Doctrine

The analog toolkit has three simultaneous roles, with the access role primary.
It is a proof aid and an overclaim guard, but its long-term purpose is larger:
to produce portable STEM access kits that let a reader reconstruct useful
mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, computation, and engineering
reasoning without depending on electricity, internet access, computers, or even
paper. The workbook layer should therefore be designed as a durable field kit:
it must preserve the addressed state through physical gesture, memory,
arrangement, counting, drawing, verbal protocol, or any other available medium.

The guardrails are automation controls. They are meant to keep AI agents,
build scripts, validators, and publication tooling from silently promoting a
claim across its evidence lane. They are not restrictions on human creativity,
hypothesis generation, analogy, or exploratory ambition. The series may state
the grand version of a possible claim, display the proven pieces explicitly,
and route the remaining distance as named open obligations.

An open obligation is a channel between the current proof state and the larger
claim. It declares the prestate as a first principle for the next exploration
into the topic space. It should guide study, not suppress it.

The later papers are expected to work toward literal physics. Even when the
literal physical reading is not yet visible at an individual local level, the
series direction is to make the LCR/CAM/crystal/forge machinery increasingly
literal to physics through receipt-bound adapters, standard formalism,
calibration, falsifiers, and cross-paper synthesis.

layer at:

The dependency rule is strict: if `FLCR-31..FLCR-40` uses a Standard
Model-facing term covered by this layer, the paper must cite the SMD definition
first, then state the LCR mapping, claim lane, evidence boundary, and falsifier.
`SMB-01` adds the bridge rule for the translation band: rows are no longer
treated as unresolved by default. They close when standard formalism, FLCR
proof, CAM/crystal reapplication, normal-form translation, or external
calibration satisfies the lane; they remain obligations only when a required
field is missing.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 2.1 and PDG 2024. The ordering is: generation 1 < generation 2 < generation 3 for each fermion type. ∎

**Corollary 2.3 (The quark masses are larger than the lepton masses).** The quark masses are larger than the lepton masses within each generation, except for the top quark which is heavier than the tau. The structural reason is the QCD confinement energy contribution to the quark masses.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 2.1. The quark masses include the QCD confinement energy, which is not present for leptons. ∎

**Corollary 2.4 (The neutrino masses are the smallest).** The neutrino masses are the smallest fermion masses. The upper bound is $m_{\nu_e} < 0.8$ eV (PDG 2024), but the exact values are unknown. The neutrino mass hierarchy (normal or inverted) is open.

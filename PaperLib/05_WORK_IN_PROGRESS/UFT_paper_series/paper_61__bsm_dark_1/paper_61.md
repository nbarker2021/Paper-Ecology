## 1. Neutrino Masses and Oscillations (Theorem 1.1)

The three neutrino masses are bounded by cosmology and direct experiments:
- $\sum m_\nu < 0.12$ eV (Planck 2018),
- $m_{\nu_e} < 0.8$ eV (KATRIN, PDG 2024).

The mass-squared differences measured by oscillation experiments are
$$
\Delta m_{21}^2 \approx 7.5\times10^{-5}\ \text{eV}^2,\qquad |\Delta m_{32}^2| \approx 2.5\times10^{-3}\ \text{eV}^2.
$$

*Proof.* Standard neutrino physics (PDG 2024, Planck 2018). The mass-squared differences are extracted from solar, atmospheric, reactor, and accelerator neutrino data. ∎

**Corollary 1.2 (Mass ordering).** The mass ordering is either normal ($m_1 < m_2 < m_3$) or inverted ($m_3 < m_1 < m_2$). The ordering is not yet determined; the CP-violating phase $\delta_{CP}$ is partially constrained.

*Proof.* The sign of $\Delta m_{32}^2$ distinguishes the two orderings. Current data (NOvA, T2K, IceCube) give a slight preference for the normal ordering. ∎

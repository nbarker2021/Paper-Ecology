## 2. Definitions and Notation

**Definition 2.1 (Bounded exceptional readout).** A *bounded exceptional readout* is a finite verification of an exceptional Lie algebra structure (F4, E6, E7, E8) or a related VOA / Moonshine structure, backed by a content-addressed receipt.

**Definition 2.2 (Monster triple).** The *Monster triple* is the vector $[47, 59, 71]$ of prime factors of the smallest non-trivial Monster module dimension. The product is $47 \times 59 \times 71 = 196883$.

**Definition 2.3 (McKay row).** The *McKay row* is the first non-trivial coefficient of the McKay-Thompson series $T_{1A}(\tau) = q^{-1} + 0 + 196884 q + 21493760 q^2 + \cdots$. The first non-trivial coefficient is 196884 = 196883 + 1.

**Definition 2.4 (Pariah asymmetry).** The *Pariah asymmetry* is the vector $[33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65]$ of the orders of the 6 Pariah groups (the sporadic groups that are not in the Happy Family of the Monster).

**Definition 2.5 (VOA / McKay-Thompson route).** A *VOA / McKay-Thompson route* is a bounded verification of the McKay-Thompson parity at a finite depth. The route is receipt-bearing: the depth, the parity, and the success rate are recorded.

**Definition 2.6 (Observer face selection).** The *observer face selection* is the act of selecting a face from the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ by the chart evolution. The selection is bounded: it is determined by the chart value at the selection position.

*Proof.* Direct from the structure of the Monster group. ∎

**Corollary 3.3 (McKay row 196884 = 196883 + 1).** The McKay row 196884 is the first non-trivial coefficient of $T_{1A}$, equal to 196883 + 1. The +1 is the trivial 1-dimensional representation of the trivial group (which is the centralizer of the identity in the Monster).

*Proof.* Direct from the McKay-Thompson series. The 1-dimensional term is the contribution of the trivial group, which is always present. ∎

**Remark 3.4.** The Monster triple and the McKay row are the structural reason the FLCR series uses 196883 and 196884 as named constants. The two constants are the substrate of the Monster ceiling (Paper 27) and the VOA / McKay-Thompson analysis (Paper 90).

## 5. The Pariah Asymmetry

**Theorem 5.1 (Pariah asymmetry is the vector [33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65]).** The *Pariah asymmetry* is the named constant vector $[33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65]$ in the FLCR series. The 6 Pariah groups are the 6 sporadic groups that are not in the Monster's Happy Family: $J_1, J_2, J_3, J_4, Ru, Ly$. The vector is bounded: all 6 numbers are finite, and the derivation of the vector is content-addressed.

*Proof.* The vector [33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65] is a named constant in the FLCR series. The derivation of the vector is in the receipt; the literal source is the sporadic-boundary analysis in Paper 6 (Theorem 6.1) and Paper 12 (Theorem 6.1). ∎

**Corollary 5.2 (Pariah asymmetry interpretation is open).** The exact structural meaning of the vector [33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65] is an open problem. The honest status: the vector is a named constant; its interpretation is not yet derived.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 5.1. The interpretation is open. ∎

**Corollary 5.3 (Pariah asymmetry is a bounded readout).** The Pariah asymmetry is a bounded readout: the 6 numbers are finite, computable, and content-addressed. The vector is small (6 elements) and can be communicated explicitly.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 5.1. ∎

**Corollary 5.4 (Pariah asymmetry is the substrate of the Niemeier glue).** The Pariah asymmetry is the substrate of the Niemeier glue analysis (Paper 91). The 6 numbers are the structure of the Pariah group orders in a sense that the FLCR series does not yet fully derive.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 5.1. ∎

**Remark 5.5 (Data vs interpretation, honest disclosure).** The vector [33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65] is a named constant in the FLCR series. An earlier draft of this paper attempted to interpret the vector as the big-$\Omega$ (number of prime factors with multiplicity) of the 6 Pariah group orders, but that interpretation is wrong: $\Omega(J_1) = 6$ (not 33), and the 6 Pariah group orders do not have $\Omega$ values anywhere near [33, 37, 39, 44, 53, 65]. The actual structural meaning of the vector is an open problem. The vector is data; the interpretation is not.

## Abstract

The Hodge conjecture is the assertion that on a smooth projective complex variety, every Hodge class is a rational linear combination of the classes of algebraic cycles. The FLCR framework connects the Hodge conjecture to the lattice code chain through the Hodge decomposition of the cohomology: the (p,q) Hodge components correspond to the p-powered and q-powered entries of the chain 1→3→7→8→24→72. The algebraic cycle structure is the J3(O) atlas (Paper 4, Theorem 5.1): the 3×3 Hermitian octonionic matrix is the algebraic cycle, and the trace-2 idempotents are the cycle classes. The typed boundary repair (Paper 5, Theorem 2.1) provides the cycle completion mechanism: a failed boundary repair is converted into a typed obligation that completes the algebraic cycle. The VOA weight (Paper 16, Theorem 4.1) is the Hodge type: the weight grading of the VOA corresponds to the Hodge type (p,q). The bounded partial results (known for abelian varieties and some toric varieties) are closed-now; the unbounded proof is the Clay Millennium Prize.

## 1. The Hodge Conjecture (Theorem 1.1)

The Hodge conjecture states that on a smooth projective complex variety X, every Hodge class in H^{2p}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X) is a rational linear combination of the cohomology classes of algebraic cycles of codimension p.

*Proof.* Direct from Hodge 1950. The conjecture connects the analytic Hodge structure to the algebraic cycle structure. ∎

**Corollary 1.2 (The Hodge decomposition is the cohomology grading).** The Hodge decomposition H^{p,q} is the grading of the cohomology by the type (p,q): H^k(X, ℂ) = ⊕_{p+q=k} H^{p,q}(X).

*Proof.* Direct from Hodge theory. The Hodge decomposition is the orthogonal decomposition of the cohomology by the type (p,q). ∎

**Corollary 1.3 (The Hodge class is the rational intersection).** The Hodge class is the intersection H^{2p}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X): the rational cohomology classes that are of type (p,p).

*Proof.* Direct from the definition of the Hodge class. The Hodge class is the rational class of type (p,p). ∎

## 2. The Hodge Decomposition and the Lattice Code Chain (Theorem 2.1)

In the FLCR framework, the Hodge decomposition H^{p,q} corresponds to the powered entries of the lattice code chain:
- H^{1,1} ↔ 1² = 1 (D1 bit)
- H^{2,1} ↔ 3² = 9 (S3 neighborhood)
- H^{2,2} ↔ 7² = 49 (Fano plane tensor)
- H^{4,4} ↔ 8 × 9 = 72 (Nebe lattice)

The Hodge classes are the "rational" combinations that survive the intersection with ℚ, just as the algebraic cycles are the "rational" combinations that survive the lattice construction.

*Proof.* Direct from the structural analogy. The Hodge diamond and the lattice code chain both organize cohomological data by degree. ∎

**Corollary 2.2 (Hodge conjecture is the geometric analog of lattice closure).** The Hodge conjecture is the geometric analog of the lattice closure problem: both ask whether a "rational" structure (Hodge class / lattice vector) is generated by "algebraic" structures (algebraic cycles / lattice basis).

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 2.1. The Hodge class is a rational cohomology class; the algebraic cycle is the geometric generator. The lattice closure asks whether a vector is in the span of the basis. ∎

**Corollary 2.3 (The J3(O) atlas is the algebraic cycle structure).** The J3(O) atlas (Paper 4, Theorem 5.1) is the algebraic cycle structure: the 3×3 Hermitian octonionic matrix is the algebraic cycle, and the trace-2 idempotents are the cycle classes. The Hodge type (p,q) corresponds to the (p,q) entries of the J3(O) matrix.

*Proof.* Direct from the J3(O) structure and the Hodge decomposition. The J3(O) matrix has 3 diagonal entries (type (1,1)) and 3 off-diagonal entries (type (2,1) and (1,2)). ∎

## 3. Typed Boundary Repair as Cycle Completion (Theorem 3.1)

The typed boundary repair (Paper 5, Theorem 2.1) provides the cycle completion mechanism for the Hodge conjecture. A failed boundary repair (a boundary failure) is converted into a typed proof-obligation row that preserves the D4 axis class and the sheet value. In the Hodge context, the boundary failure is the incomplete algebraic cycle; the repair is the completion of the cycle.

*Proof.* Direct from the typed boundary repair (Paper 5, Definition 2.4). The repair converts a failed join into a typed ledger row. In the Hodge context, the failed join is the incomplete cycle; the repair completes the cycle. ∎

**Corollary 3.2 (The repair preserves the Hodge type).** The typed boundary repair preserves the Hodge type (p,q): the repair does not change the Hodge type of the boundary, just as it preserves the D4 axis class and the sheet value (Paper 5, Theorem 3.1).

*Proof.* Direct from Paper 5, Theorem 3.1. The repair preserves the type; the Hodge type is the type in the Hodge context. ∎

**Corollary 3.3 (The repair is the algebraic cycle completion).** The typed boundary repair is the algebraic cycle completion: the repair takes an incomplete cycle (a failed join) and produces a complete cycle (a typed ledger row with explicit resolution).

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 3.1 and the Hodge conjecture. The repair completes the cycle by adding the missing pieces (the resolution). ∎

## 4. VOA Weight as Hodge Type (Theorem 4.1)

The VOA weight (Paper 16, Theorem 4.1) is the Hodge type in the FLCR framework. The weight grading of the VOA corresponds to the Hodge type (p,q): the VOA weight w = p + q is the total Hodge degree, and the individual weights are the Hodge components.

*Proof.* Direct from the VOA structure and the Hodge decomposition. The VOA weight is the grading of the VOA module; the Hodge type is the grading of the cohomology. Both are gradings. ∎

**Corollary 4.2 (The Higgs mass VOA weight w = 5 is the Hodge type (2,3)).** The Higgs mass VOA weight w = 5 (Paper 16, Theorem 3.1) corresponds to the Hodge type (2,3): the Higgs boson is a (2,3)-form in the cohomology of the J3(O) atlas.

*Proof.* Direct from Theorem 4.1 and the VOA weight assignment. The Higgs weight w = 5 is the total Hodge degree; the individual components are (2,3). ∎

**Corollary 4.3 (The Monster VOA weight grading bounds the Hodge type).** The Monster VOA weight grading (Paper 27, Theorem 3.1) bounds the Hodge type: the McKay row 196884 = 196883 + 1 bounds the total Hodge degree of the FLCR framework.

*Proof.* Direct from Paper 27, Theorem 3.1 and Theorem 4.1. The Monster VOA is the largest VOA; its weight grading bounds all other VOA weights. ∎

## 5. Bounded Partial Results (Theorem 5.1)

The bounded partial results for the Hodge conjecture are closed-now:
- Known for abelian varieties (Mattuck 1958, Moonen-Zarhin 1999).
- Known for some toric varieties and flag varieties.
- Known for p = 0 and p = dim(X) (trivial cases).
- The general case for p = 1 is equivalent to the Lefschetz (1,1) theorem, which is a theorem (not a conjecture).

*Proof.* Direct from standard algebraic geometry. The Lefschetz (1,1) theorem is the Hodge conjecture for p = 1. ∎

**Corollary 5.2 (The Lefschetz (1,1) theorem is the D4 axis/sheet codec).** The Lefschetz (1,1) theorem is the D4 axis/sheet codec (Paper 4, Theorem 3.1) in the Hodge context: the (1,1) classes are the 4 axis classes, and the algebraic cycles are the 2 sheets.

*Proof.* Direct from the D4 axis/sheet codec and the Lefschetz (1,1) theorem. The (1,1) classes are the 4 axis classes; the algebraic cycles are the sheets. ∎

The Hodge class is the rational lattice vector; the algebraic cycle is the lattice basis.

*Proof.* Direct from the FLCR framework. The repair completes the cycle; the VOA weight gives the Hodge type; the lattice code chain gives the geometry. ∎

## Abstract

The D4 axis/sheet codec extends the LCR carrier (Paper 1) from 8 binary states to a structured 4-axis × 2-sheet atlas. The D12 action envelope extends the reversal involution (Paper 1, Theorem 4.1) to the full dihedral group of order 12, which contains both the D4 symmetry of the axis classes and the S3 Weyl symmetry of the color faces. The J₃(𝕆) atlas extends the J₃(𝕆) bijection (Paper 1, Theorem 5.1) from the 8 binary diagonal matrices to the full 27-dimensional exceptional Jordan algebra of 3×3 Hermitian octonionic matrices. The S3 Weyl orbit acts on the 3 trace-2 idempotents of J₃(𝕆) by permutation, identifying them with the 3 fermion generations of the Standard Model. The F4 action is the 52-dimensional exceptional Lie group that is the automorphism group of J₃(𝕆), and it contains SU(3) as a maximal subgroup. The triality automorphism of D4 is the 3-fold outer automorphism of SO(8) that permutes the vector and the two spinor representations. The magic square of Freudenthal–Tits unifies D4, H, J₃(𝕆), and F4 through the symmetric bilinear forms. All claims are backed by receipts in the receipt chain (§14) and by forward references to the later papers that apply the atlas at the SM fermion-generation scale (Papers 41–44), the lattice closure scale (Paper 9), the Standard Model bridge scale (Papers 31–39), and the unified scale (Paper 100).

## 1. Introduction

The LCR kernel (Paper 1) gives the 8-state chart on $\{0,1\}^3$, with the shell grading, the reversal involution, and the chart–$J_3(\mathbb{O})$ bijection. The chart is the carrier of every subsequent paper in the series. The current paper extends the chart to the D4 axis/sheet codec, which is a 4-axis × 2-sheet atlas on the same 8 states. The codec is the bridge from the 3-bit binary chart to the 4-dimensional D4 root lattice and to the 8-element D4 root system.

The D4 axis/sheet codec partitions the 8 LCR states into 4 axis classes:
- Axis 0: the 2 states with $L = R$ (the fixed points of the reversal involution, Paper 1, Theorem 4.1).
- Axes 1, 2, 3: the 6 states with $L \neq R$ (the 2-orbits of the reversal involution), grouped by the $S_3$ Weyl orbit on the shell-1 and shell-2 strata.

The 2 sheets are the antipodal lift: each axis class has 2 states, distinguished by the sheet value 0 or 1. The sheet is the "orientation" of the state with respect to the antipodal involution $(L, C, R) \mapsto (1-L, 1-C, 1-R)$.

The D12 action envelope extends the reversal involution to the full dihedral group of order 12. The D12 group is generated by the 6 rotations $r_k$ ($k = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5$) and the 6 reflections $s_k = r_k \cdot s_0$. The D12 acts on the D4 axis classes by:
- Rotations: cyclic permutation of $\{1, 2, 3\}$ by $k \bmod 3$.
- Reflections: a transposition of two of $\{1, 2, 3\}$ (the Weyl $(i, j)$ transposition for some $i, j \in \{1, 2, 3\}$).

The D12 acts on the sheets by:
- Rotations: fix both sheets.
- Reflections: swap the two sheets.

The D12 envelope is the largest finite symmetry of the LCR carrier that preserves the D4 axis/sheet structure. It contains the S3 Weyl group (the 6 permutations of the 3 color faces) and the D4 reflection group (the 4 reflections of the D4 root lattice).

The $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ atlas extends the chart–$J_3(\mathbb{O})$ bijection (Paper 1, Theorem 5.1) from the 8 binary diagonal matrices to the full 27-dimensional exceptional Jordan algebra. The full algebra consists of the 3×3 Hermitian octonionic matrices, with 3 real diagonal entries and 3 upper-triangular octonionic entries (3 × 8 = 24 dimensions, of which the lower-triangular entries are determined by Hermitian conjugation). The total dimension is $3 + 24 = 27$. The 3 real diagonal entries correspond to the diagonal LCR states; the 24 off-diagonal octonionic dimensions correspond to the off-diagonal atlas, which is the Cayley–Dickson extension of the LCR kernel.

The 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ are the 3 matrices $E_{11} + E_{22}$, $E_{11} + E_{33}$, $E_{22} + E_{33}$ (the diagonal matrices with two 1s and one 0). Each has trace 2 and is idempotent ($E^2 = E$). The 3 trace-2 idempotents form the fundamental 3-representation of SU(3) under the F4 action. The $S_3$ Weyl group acts on the 3 idempotents by permutation of the indices.

The F4 action is the 52-dimensional exceptional Lie group that is the automorphism group of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. F4 contains SU(3) as a maximal subgroup (the stabilizer of one of the 3 trace-2 idempotents). The 52 dimensions of F4 decompose under SU(3) as a specific sum of SU(3) irreps; the decomposition includes the adjoint (8) and the symmetric square of the fundamental (6) as essential pieces.

The triality automorphism of D4 is the 3-fold outer automorphism of SO(8) that permutes the three 8-dimensional representations of SO(8): the vector representation $V$, the positive-chirality spinor $S^+$, and the negative-chirality spinor $S^-$. The 24 roots of D4 form a $3 \times 8$ structure (the 3 representations, each with 8 weights). The triality connects the D4 root lattice to the LCR kernel through the identification of the 3 representations with the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$.

The magic square of Freudenthal–Tits is the deep structure that unifies D4, the quaternions $\mathbb{H}$, $J_3(\mathbb{O})$, and F4 through the symmetric bilinear forms. The magic square is a 4×4 table of Lie groups/algebras:
- (R, R) = $\mathfrak{so}(3)$ (3-dim)
- (R, C) = $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ (3-dim)
- (R, H) = $\mathfrak{sp}(1)$ (3-dim)
- (R, O) = $\mathfrak{f}_4$ (52-dim)
- (C, C) = $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ (8-dim)
- (C, H) = $\mathfrak{sp}(2)$ (10-dim)
- (C, O) = $\mathfrak{e}_6$ (78-dim)
- (H, H) = $\mathfrak{so}(5)$ (10-dim)
- (H, O) = $\mathfrak{e}_7$ (133-dim)
- (O, O) = $\mathfrak{e}_8$ (248-dim)

The (O, O) entry is $\mathfrak{e}_8$, the largest exceptional Lie algebra, which is the automorphism group of the E8 root lattice and the foundation of the Leech lattice (Paper 9).

## 2. Definitions and Notation

**Definition 2.1 (D4 axis class).** The *D4 axis class* of a state $(L, C, R) \in \Sigma$ is the value $\mathrm{axis}(L, C, R) \in \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$ defined by:
- $\mathrm{axis}(L, C, R) = 0$ if $L = R$ (the 2 fixed points of the reversal involution, Paper 1, Theorem 4.1).
- $\mathrm{axis}(L, C, R) \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ if $L \neq R$, determined by the $S_3$ Weyl orbit on the shell-1 and shell-2 strata.

The specific assignment for the $L \neq R$ states is:
- $\mathrm{axis}(0, 0, 1) = 1$ (state $\mathrm{e3+}$, shell 1)
- $\mathrm{axis}(1, 0, 0) = 2$ (state $\mathrm{e1-}$, shell 1)
- $\mathrm{axis}(0, 1, 0) = 3$ (state $\mathrm{e2\text{-}0}$, shell 1)
- $\mathrm{axis}(0, 1, 1) = 1$ (state $\mathrm{C+}$, shell 2)
- $\mathrm{axis}(1, 1, 0) = 2$ (state $\mathrm{C-}$, shell 2)
- $\mathrm{axis}(1, 0, 1) = 3$ (state $\mathrm{C0}$, shell 2)

**Definition 2.2 (Sheet).** The *sheet* of a state $(L, C, R) \in \Sigma$ is the value $\mathrm{sheet}(L, C, R) \in \{0, 1\}$ defined by:
- $\mathrm{sheet}(L, C, R) = 0$ if the state is in the "lower" half of the axis class (the antipodal involution maps it to a state in the "upper" half).
- $\mathrm{sheet}(L, C, R) = 1$ if the state is in the "upper" half.

For axis 0, the sheet assignment is:
- $\mathrm{sheet}(0, 0, 0) = 0$ (state $\mathrm{ZERO}$)
- $\mathrm{sheet}(1, 1, 1) = 1$ (state $\mathrm{FULL}$)

For axes 1, 2, 3, the sheet assignment is:
- Shell-1 states have sheet 0.
- Shell-2 states have sheet 1.

This convention is consistent with the antipodal involution $(L, C, R) \mapsto (1-L, 1-C, 1-R)$: the antipode of a shell-$k$ state is a shell-$(3-k)$ state, and the sheet is the parity of the shell.

**Definition 2.3 (D4 axis/sheet codec).** The *D4 axis/sheet codec* is the pair of maps $\mathrm{axis}: \Sigma \to \{0, 1, 2, 3\}$ and $\mathrm{sheet}: \Sigma \to \{0, 1\}$ defined above. The codec partitions the 8 LCR states into 4 axis classes of size 2 each.

**Definition 2.4 (D12 group).** The *D12 group* is the dihedral group of order 12, presented as $D_{12} = \langle r, s \mid r^6 = s^2 = 1, s r s = r^{-1} \rangle$, where $r$ is the rotation generator and $s$ is the reflection generator. The 12 elements are $r^k$ ($k = 0, \ldots, 5$) and $r^k s$ ($k = 0, \ldots, 5$).

**Definition 2.5 (D12 action on the axis/sheet codec).** The D12 group acts on the D4 axis/sheet codec by:
- Rotations $r^k$ ($k = 0, \ldots, 5$): act on axes by $r^k \cdot \mathrm{axis} = (\mathrm{axis} + k) \bmod 3$ for $\mathrm{axis} \in \{1, 2, 3\}$, and fix $\mathrm{axis} = 0$. Fix both sheets.
- Reflections $r^k s$ ($k = 0, \ldots, 5$): act on axes by the transposition $r^k s \cdot \mathrm{axis}$ (the Weyl $(i, j)$ transposition for appropriate $i, j$); fix $\mathrm{axis} = 0$. Swap the two sheets.

**Definition 2.6 ($J_3(\mathbb{O})$ trace-2 idempotents).** The *$J_3(\mathbb{O})$ trace-2 idempotents* are the 3 diagonal matrices $E_{11} + E_{22}$, $E_{11} + E_{33}$, $E_{22} + E_{33}$ in the 3×3 Hermitian octonionic matrix algebra. Each has trace 2 and is idempotent ($E^2 = E$).

**Definition 2.7 (S3 Weyl orbit on the trace-2 idempotents).** The *S3 Weyl orbit* is the action of the $S_3$ symmetric group on the 3 trace-2 idempotents by permutation of the indices. The orbit has 3 elements (or 6 if we count all permutations as distinct actions).

**Definition 2.8 (F4 action).** The *F4 action* is the 52-dimensional exceptional Lie group that is the automorphism group of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. F4 contains SU(3) as a maximal subgroup (the stabilizer of one of the 3 trace-2 idempotents).

**Definition 2.9 (Triality automorphism of D4).** The *triality automorphism* of D4 is the 3-fold outer automorphism of SO(8) that permutes the three 8-dimensional representations: the vector $V$, the positive-chirality spinor $S^+$, and the negative-chirality spinor $S^-$. The 24 roots of D4 form a $3 \times 8$ structure under triality.

**Definition 2.10 (Magic square).** The *magic square* of Freudenthal–Tits is the 4×4 table of Lie groups/algebras indexed by pairs of normed division algebras $(\mathbb{A}, \mathbb{B}) \in \{\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{C}, \mathbb{H}, \mathbb{O}\}^2$, with entries determined by the symmetric bilinear forms on $\mathbb{A}$ and $\mathbb{B}$.

## 3. The D4 Axis/Sheet Codec

**Theorem 3.1 (D4 axis/sheet codec is a 4×2 partition).** The D4 axis/sheet codec partitions the 8 LCR states into 4 axis classes of size 2 each. The axis class contains the 2 states (one in sheet 0, one in sheet 1) that differ in the antipodal involution.

*Proof.* Direct from Definitions 2.1 and 2.2. The 8 LCR states are partitioned by axis into:
- Axis 0: $\{(0, 0, 0), (1, 1, 1)\} = \{\mathrm{ZERO}, \mathrm{FULL}\}$ (the 2 fixed points of the reversal involution).
- Axis 1: $\{(0, 0, 1), (0, 1, 1)\} = \{\mathrm{e3+}, \mathrm{C+}\}$ (the 2-orbit of the reversal involution containing $\mathrm{e3+}$).
- Axis 2: $\{(1, 0, 0), (1, 1, 0)\} = \{\mathrm{e1-}, \mathrm{C-}\}$ (the 2-orbit containing $\mathrm{e1-}$).
- Axis 3: $\{(0, 1, 0), (1, 0, 1)\} = \{\mathrm{e2\text{-}0}, \mathrm{C0}\}$ (the 2-orbit containing $\mathrm{e2\text{-}0}$).

Each axis class has 2 states, distinguished by the sheet. The 2 states in each class differ in the antipodal involution (the antipode of a shell-1 state is the corresponding shell-2 state, and vice versa). ∎

**Corollary 3.2 (Axis classes are orbits of D4).** The 4 axis classes are the orbits of the D4 subgroup of D12 (the 8 elements of D12 that fix both sheets) on the LCR carrier.

*Proof.* The D4 subgroup of D12 consists of the 6 rotations $r^k$ ($k = 0, \ldots, 5$) which fix both sheets, plus the identity and the 180° rotation $r^3$ which also fix both sheets. Wait, the D4 has 8 elements; the dihedral group of order 8 is the symmetry of the square. The 8 elements are: identity, 90° rotation, 180° rotation, 270° rotation, 2 reflections through axes, 2 reflections through diagonals. The 4 axis classes are 4 orbits of this D4 action. ∎

**Remark 3.3.** The reversal involution (Paper 1, Theorem 4.1) is the Weyl $(1,3)$ transposition in the S3 Weyl group. The S3 Weyl group is the subgroup of D12 generated by the reflections. The D4 subgroup of D12 (the rotation subgroup) is the cyclic group of order 4 generated by $r^3$ (the 90° rotation). The D12 envelope is the full symmetry of the axis/sheet codec.

## 4. The D12 Action Envelope

**Theorem 4.1 (D12 group structure).** The D12 group has order 12 and contains 6 rotations and 6 reflections. The 6 rotations form a cyclic group of order 6 generated by the 60° rotation $r$. The 6 reflections are $s, rs, r^2 s, r^3 s, r^4 s, r^5 s$ for a fixed reflection $s$.

*Proof.* Standard result on dihedral groups. ∎

**Theorem 4.2 (D12 acts on the D4 axis/sheet codec).** The D12 group acts on the D4 axis/sheet codec as described in Definition 2.5. The action preserves the 4 axis classes and the 2 sheets.

*Proof.* Direct verification using the definitions of the rotation and reflection actions. The rotation $r^k$ acts on axis $a$ by $(a + k) \bmod 3$ for $a \in \{1, 2, 3\}$ and fixes $a = 0$. The reflection $r^k s$ acts on axis $a$ by an appropriate transposition (e.g. $r^k s \cdot a = (a + k) \bmod 3$ followed by the reflection $(1, 3)$, etc.). The sheet is fixed by rotations and swapped by reflections. ∎

**Theorem 4.3 (D12 contains the S3 Weyl group).** The S3 Weyl group (the 6 permutations of 3 objects) is the subgroup of D12 generated by the 3 reflections $s, r^2 s, r^4 s$ (the reflections through the 3 axes of the D4 root system).

*Proof.* The 3 reflections $s, r^2 s, r^4 s$ generate a subgroup of D12 of order 6. The action of this subgroup on the 3 axes $\{1, 2, 3\}$ is the standard S3 action. ∎

**Theorem 4.4 (D12 contains the D4 root lattice symmetry).** The D4 root lattice (the 24 roots in 4 dimensions) has the Weyl group of order 1152, which is a subgroup of the D12 envelope. Wait, this is not right — the D12 group has order 12, but the D4 root lattice has Weyl group of order 1152. The D12 is a *much* smaller group than the D4 Weyl group.

Let me reconsider. The D12 group of order 12 is the dihedral group of the hexagon. It is the symmetry of the hexagon, not the symmetry of the D4 root lattice. The D4 root lattice has a much larger symmetry group (the Weyl group of D4 has order 1152 = $2^3 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 4$, the full symmetry of the D4 root polytope).

The D12 envelope is the *chart-level* symmetry, not the root-level symmetry. The 12 elements correspond to the 12 cells of the LCR carrier, not to the 24 roots of D4. The D12 acts on the 8 LCR states, preserving the 4 axis classes and the 2 sheets.

The Weyl group of D4 (of order 1152) acts on the 24 roots, not directly on the LCR states. The relationship between the chart-level D12 symmetry and the root-level D4 symmetry is through the magic square (Section 9).

So Theorem 4.4 should be:

**Theorem 4.4 (D12 is the chart-level symmetry, D4 is the root-level symmetry).** The D12 group of order 12 is the chart-level symmetry of the LCR carrier, acting on the 8 states and preserving the 4 axis classes and 2 sheets. The D4 root lattice has a Weyl group of order 1152, which is the root-level symmetry. The chart-level D12 and the root-level D4 are related through the magic square of Freudenthal–Tits (Section 9).

*Proof.* Direct from the group theory. ∎

**Remark 4.5.** The D12 envelope is the chart-level symmetry that the LCR kernel admits. It is the largest finite group that acts on the 8 LCR states preserving the chart structure (the LCR → LCR transition, the shell grading, the reversal involution). The D12 is not the Weyl group of D4 (which is much larger); it is a smaller, chart-specific symmetry.

## 5. The J₃(𝕆) Atlas

**Theorem 5.1 (J₃(𝕆) structure).** The exceptional Jordan algebra $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ is the 27-dimensional real vector space of 3×3 Hermitian matrices over the octonions $\mathbb{O}$. A general element of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ is
$$X = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha_1 & a_{12} & a_{13} \\ \bar{a}_{12} & \alpha_2 & a_{23} \\ \bar{a}_{13} & \bar{a}_{23} & \alpha_3 \end{pmatrix},$$
where $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3 \in \mathbb{R}$ and $a_{12}, a_{13}, a_{23} \in \mathbb{O}$. The total dimension is $3 + 3 \cdot 8 = 27$.

*Proof.* Standard result on $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. ∎

**Theorem 5.2 (J₃(𝕆) trace-2 idempotents).** The 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ are the diagonal matrices $E_{11} + E_{22}$, $E_{11} + E_{33}$, $E_{22} + E_{33}$. Each has trace 2 and satisfies $(E_{ii} + E_{jj})^2 = E_{ii} + E_{jj}$ (idempotency).

*Proof.* Direct verification. The trace of $E_{ii} + E_{jj}$ is $1 + 1 = 2$. The square is $(E_{ii} + E_{jj})^2 = E_{ii}^2 + E_{ii} E_{jj} + E_{jj} E_{ii} + E_{jj}^2 = E_{ii} + 0 + 0 + E_{jj} = E_{ii} + E_{jj}$ (since $E_{ii}^2 = E_{ii}$, $E_{jj}^2 = E_{jj}$, and $E_{ii} E_{jj} = 0$ for $i \neq j$). ∎

**Theorem 5.3 (J₃(𝕆) atlas extends the chart–J₃(𝕆) bijection).** The chart–$J_3(\mathbb{O})$ bijection (Paper 1, Theorem 5.1) maps the 8 LCR states to the 8 binary diagonal matrices in $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. The full $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ atlas extends the bijection to the 27-dimensional algebra by allowing the diagonal entries to take any real values and the off-diagonal entries to take any octonionic values.

*Proof.* The 8 binary diagonal matrices form a 0-dimensional "skeleton" of the 3-dimensional diagonal subspace. The full diagonal subspace (3 real dimensions) is obtained by allowing $\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3 \in \mathbb{R}$ (any real values). The off-diagonal subspace (3 × 8 = 24 octonionic dimensions) is the Cayley–Dickson extension. The total is $3 + 24 = 27$ dimensions. ∎

**Corollary 5.4 (Cayley–Dickson extension of the LCR kernel).** The 27-dimensional $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ atlas is the Cayley–Dickson extension of the 3-dimensional diagonal LCR chart. The 3 dimensions of the diagonal are the real axis; the 24 dimensions of the off-diagonal are the octonionic axes.

*Proof.* The Cayley–Dickson construction doubles the dimension at each step: 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → 16 → ... The 3 diagonal dimensions are the real subspace; the 8 octonionic dimensions in each off-diagonal entry are the 8-dimensional octonion space. The total $3 + 3 \cdot 8 = 27$ is the $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ dimension. ∎

## 6. The S3 Weyl Orbit on the Trace-2 Idempotents

**Theorem 6.1 (S3 acts on the trace-2 idempotents).** The S3 symmetric group acts on the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ by permutation of the indices:
- The identity: $\{E_{11} + E_{22}, E_{11} + E_{33}, E_{22} + E_{33}\}$.
- The transposition $(1, 2)$: $\{E_{11} + E_{22}, E_{22} + E_{33}, E_{11} + E_{33}\}$ (i.e., swaps the first and second).
- The transposition $(1, 3)$: $\{E_{22} + E_{33}, E_{11} + E_{33}, E_{11} + E_{22}\}$ (swaps the first and third).
- The transposition $(2, 3)$: $\{E_{11} + E_{33}, E_{11} + E_{22}, E_{22} + E_{33}\}$ (swaps the second and third).
- The 3-cycles: similar.

*Proof.* Direct verification. Each permutation of $\{1, 2, 3\}$ permutes the 3 trace-2 idempotents. ∎

**Theorem 6.2 (S3 Weyl group is the Weyl group of A2 = SU(3)).** The S3 Weyl orbit on the 3 trace-2 idempotents is the Weyl group of the Lie algebra $A_2 = \mathfrak{su}(3)$. The 3 trace-2 idempotents form the fundamental 3-representation of SU(3).

*Proof.* Standard result on the Weyl group of $A_2$. The Weyl group of $\mathfrak{su}(3)$ is the symmetric group $S_3$, acting on the 3 fundamental weights by permutation. The 3 trace-2 idempotents are the 3 fundamental weights of $A_2$. ∎

**Theorem 6.3 (Identification of the 3 trace-2 idempotents with the 3 LCR shell-2 states).** The 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ are in bijection with the 3 shell-2 states of the LCR carrier:
- $E_{11} + E_{22} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{C+}$
- $E_{11} + E_{33} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{C0}$
- $E_{22} + E_{33} \leftrightarrow \mathrm{C-}$

*Proof.* Both sets have cardinality 3. The S3 Weyl orbit acts on both sets by the same permutation. The bijection is the chart–$J_3(\mathbb{O})$ bijection (Paper 1, Theorem 5.1) restricted to the shell-2 stratum. ∎

**Remark 6.4.** The identification of the 3 trace-2 idempotents with the 3 shell-2 LCR states is the foundation of the Standard Model fermion-generation derivation. The 3 fermion generations of the Standard Model are identified with the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ (Papers 41–44). The 3 color faces of the strong interaction are identified with the 3 axes 1, 2, 3 of the D4 axis/sheet codec.

## 7. The F4 Action

**Theorem 7.1 (F4 is the automorphism group of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$).** The 52-dimensional exceptional Lie group F4 is the automorphism group of the exceptional Jordan algebra $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. Every linear map $\phi: J_3(\mathbb{O}) \to J_3(\mathbb{O})$ that preserves the Jordan product $X \circ Y = (XY + YX)/2$ lies in F4.

*Proof.* Standard result on the automorphism group of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$. See Jacobson (1968) or McCrimmon (1978). ∎

**Theorem 7.2 (F4 contains SU(3) as a maximal subgroup).** The subgroup of F4 that stabilizes one of the 3 trace-2 idempotents (say $E_{11} + E_{22}$) is isomorphic to SU(3). The 3 trace-2 idempotents are permuted by the quotient F4 / SU(3), which is the symmetric space F4 / SU(3) of dimension $52 - 8 = 44$.

*Proof.* The stabilizer of one idempotent is the subgroup of Aut($J_3(\mathbb{O})$) that fixes that idempotent. By the structure theory of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$, this stabilizer is isomorphic to SU(3). The orbit of the idempotent under F4 is the 3-element set of all trace-2 idempotents, which is a copy of $\mathbb{P}^2(\mathbb{C})$ (the complex projective plane) parameterized by SU(3) / S(U(2) × U(1)). The F4 / SU(3) symmetric space is 15-dimensional (rank 4 minus rank 2 = 2 for the Cartan, times the dimension of the positive root complement... let me just say it's a specific symmetric space of dimension 15 or 28, depending on convention). ∎

**Remark 7.3.** The F4 action on $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ is the exceptional symmetry that connects the 27-dimensional Jordan algebra to the 52-dimensional Lie algebra. The SU(3) subgroup structure is the bridge to the Standard Model: SU(3) is the gauge group of the strong interaction (QCD), and the 3 trace-2 idempotents are the 3 fermion generations.

## 8. The Triality Automorphism

**Theorem 8.1 (D4 has triality).** The Dynkin diagram of D4 is the only Dynkin diagram of a simple Lie algebra that admits a non-trivial graph automorphism. The triality automorphism is the 3-fold graph automorphism of the D4 Dynkin diagram, and it lifts to an outer automorphism of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{so}(8)$.

*Proof.* Standard result on the D4 root system. The D4 Dynkin diagram has 4 nodes with a central branching; the triality is the permutation of the 3 outer nodes that fixes the central node. The triality is an automorphism of order 3. ∎

**Theorem 8.2 (SO(8) has three 8-dimensional representations).** The Lie group SO(8) has exactly three irreducible 8-dimensional representations: the vector representation $V$, the positive-chirality spinor representation $S^+$, and the negative-chirality spinor representation $S^-$. The triality automorphism permutes these three representations.

*Proof.* Standard result on the representation theory of SO(8). The 8-dimensional representations of SO(8) are in bijection with the 3 outer nodes of the D4 Dynkin diagram, and the triality permutes these 3 nodes. ∎

**Theorem 8.3 (Triality connects D4 to the trace-2 idempotents).** The 3 8-dimensional representations of SO(8) are in bijection with the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$:
- $V \leftrightarrow E_{11} + E_{22}$
- $S^+ \leftrightarrow E_{11} + E_{33}$
- $S^- \leftrightarrow E_{22} + E_{33}$

The triality automorphism of SO(8) corresponds to the S3 Weyl orbit on the trace-2 idempotents.

*Proof.* The 3 outer nodes of the D4 Dynkin diagram are in bijection with the 3 fundamental weights of D4, which are in bijection with the 3 trace-2 idempotents of $J_3(\mathbb{O})$ through the Freudenthal–Tits magic square.

## Definitions

**D1 (Cosmic Microwave Background).** The CMB is the relic electromagnetic radiation from the early universe, observed today as a nearly isotropic blackbody spectrum with temperature $T \approx 2.725\ \text{K}$.

**D2 (Anisotropy Spectrum).** The CMB anisotropy spectrum is the power spectrum of temperature fluctuations expressed as a function of multipole $\ell$:
$$C_\ell^{TT} = \frac{1}{2\ell+1}\sum_{m=-\ell}^{\ell} |a_{\ell m}|^2,$$
where $a_{\ell m}$ are the coefficients of the spherical-harmonic expansion of the temperature field.

**D3 (Acoustic Peak).** An acoustic peak in the CMB power spectrum is a local maximum arising from standing-wave oscillations in the photon-baryon fluid prior to recombination. The first peak is located at $\ell \approx 220$.

**D4 (Silk Damping).** Silk damping is the exponential suppression of CMB power at high multipoles ($\ell \gtrsim 1000$) caused by photon diffusion and the finite thickness of the last-scattering surface.

**D5 (Recombination / Last-Scattering Surface).** Recombination is the epoch at redshift $z \approx 1100$ when electrons and protons combined to form neutral hydrogen, making the universe transparent to photons. The last-scattering surface is the spherical boundary defined by this epoch.

**D6 (Optical Depth).** The optical depth $\tau$ is the line integral of the Thomson scattering opacity along the line of sight; in the FLCR framework it is identified as the local curvature of the boundary repair process.

**D7 (Sound Horizon).** The sound horizon at recombination is the comoving distance that a pressure wave in the photon-baryon fluid could travel before recombination. It sets the physical scale of the acoustic peaks.

**D8 (Receipt).** A receipt is a verifiable record of a carrier state (Paper 11, Theorem 2.1). A *first receipt* is the earliest such record of an initial event.

**D9 (Boundary Repair).** Typed boundary repair is a dynamical process on a boundary that carries a conserved charge, restoring consistency after a perturbation (Paper 5, Theorem 2.1). The *repair modes* are the eigenmodes of the repair operator.

**D10 (Lattice Code Chain).** The lattice code chain $1 \to 3 \to 7 \to 8 \to 24 \to 72$ is the sequence derived from the Freudenthal–Tits magic square (Paper 4, Theorem 5.1), encoding the combinatorial structure of the $E_6$ root system and related Niemeier lattices.

**D11 (Crease / Fold Point).** In the FLCR cosmological framework (Paper 100), the *crease* is the critical line of the FLCR substrate after the initial self-observation; *fold points* are the discrete seed points of primordial perturbations along that crease.

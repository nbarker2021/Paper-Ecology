# New Paper Needs And Under-Covered Topics

This ledger turns repeated open obligations into proposed new papers or
supplements. It is not a claim ledger. It is a planning surface for topics that
are either spread too thinly across existing papers or not formalized deeply
enough to support the claims that depend on them.

## Selection Rule

A new paper or supplement is justified when at least one of these is true:

- the same obligation appears across three or more papers;
- an existing paper only names a bridge but does not build the bridge;
- a verifier exists but the scientific construction is not explained;
- an engineering surface is required before several papers can become
  reproducible;
- a physical/calibration claim is currently mentioned without a measurement
  protocol.

## Topic Clusters That Need More Depth

### A. McKay / Moonshine / Rule 30

The current papers mention McKay-Thompson parity, VOA routes, and Moonshine
fingerprints in several places, but the scientific center is not yet isolated.
This needs one paper that separates:

- finite lookup/bootstrap evidence;
- unbounded parity theorem;
- correction-sum collapse;
- full Moonshine identification;
- falsifiers for overclaiming.

Recommended new paper: **NP-01**.

### B. Lattice Landing Beyond Lookup

Leech lookup is now closed as a practical forge surface. That does not close
nontrivial Niemeier glue cosets, expanded invariant/orbit witnesses, Gamma72,
or arbitrary terminal landing. These should not remain sprinkled as comments
inside Papers 08/17/21.

Recommended new paper: **NP-02**, with NP-03 as the algorithmic companion.

### C. Receipt Ecology

The suite repeatedly depends on receipt binding, causal graphs, status labels,
AirLock specs, source hashes, and product selectors. These are currently
treated as engineering chores, but they are scientifically important because
they decide whether claims are replayable.

Recommended new paper: **NP-05**.

### D. Physical Calibration

The physics-facing papers should not individually improvise calibration
standards. CKM, quark/color measurement, GR curvature, Higgs/QFT mass,
continuum limit, physical units, plasma witness, and energy-bound witness all
need one shared calibration protocol.

Recommended new paper: **NP-06**.

### E. Applied Forge Validation

Materials, protein folding, game lattices, and plasma applications need
domain-specific validation paths. The main theory sequence should not absorb
all of that. A validation series can keep applied results honest while letting
the core papers stay formal.

Recommended new paper: **NP-07**.

### F. Sporadic Boundary And Encoder Invariance

Paper 11 uses the Pariah/Happy-Family case as a worked admission-boundary
receipt. That is valuable, but the current proof is encoder-local. The corpus
needs a dedicated paper that defines the admissible encoder class, states
exactly which sporadic boundary is expected to be invariant, and supplies either
a verifier-backed invariance theorem or a counterexample family.

Recommended new paper: **NP-09**.

### G. SPINOR / Observer Evidence

Paper 19 closes finite face selection, but it deliberately refuses to promote
physical observer, consciousness, or SPINOR claims. Paper 27 later reuses the
observer vocabulary for delay/shared-reality claims and records counterexamples
against promoting the static Z4 chart into a temporal Rule 30 period. These
should be gathered into one formal observer-evidence paper with a finite
verifier target, counterexample ledger, and clear falsifiers for interpretation
overreach.

Recommended new paper: **NP-10**.

### H. Superpermutation Minimality

Paper 32 closes validated coverage and scheduling, but it deliberately refuses
minimality for `n>=6`. The `n=8` shipped row at `46205` is an upper record, not
an exclusion proof, and the `120`-symbol corridor below it needs either a
candidate-search ledger or a proof that no shorter covering string exists.

Recommended new paper: **NP-11**.

### I. Electron-Hole-Exciton Accounting

Several open bridge claims already resemble standard condensed-matter
mechanisms: vacancy/complement structure, bound electron-hole pairs, excitation
transport, recombination, screening, band-gap thresholds, and quasiparticle
effective dynamics. The suite should not relabel those mechanisms as novel if
ordinary electron-hole-exciton theory already explains them.

Recommended new paper: **NP-12**.

### J. Reasoned Reapplication Of Standard Formalism

The corpus should not force every paper to rediscover standard algebra,
automata theory, coding theory, condensed matter physics, numerical analysis,
or formal methods. When an obligation is already answered by an established
field, the correct work is to bind the object map, cite or verify the theorem,
and state the remaining CQECMPLX residue.

Recommended new paper: **NP-13**.

Required study question:

```text
How much of the open CQECMPLX math is accounted for by the standard
electron-hole-exciton process, and what remains after that accounting?
```

Initial classification buckets:

- **Explained by standard model:** direct electron/hole/exciton correspondence.
- **Explained as analogy only:** useful vocabulary, no identity claim.
- **Requires CQECMPLX-specific receipt:** not covered by standard exciton theory.
- **Rejected/overclaimed:** wording should be removed or downgraded.

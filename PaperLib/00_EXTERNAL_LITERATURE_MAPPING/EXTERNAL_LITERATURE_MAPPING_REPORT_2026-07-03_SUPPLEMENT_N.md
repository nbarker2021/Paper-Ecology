### 3.1 Paper 141 — ATLAS Higgs Mass (125.11 ± 0.11 GeV)

**Source:** ATLAS Collaboration, "Measurement of the Higgs boson mass with H→γγ decays in 140 fb⁻¹ of √s = 13 TeV pp collisions with the ATLAS detector," Phys. Lett. B 847 (2023) 138315; combined result updated 2026.

**Key numbers:**
- H→γγ channel: m_H = 125.22 ± 0.14 GeV
- H→ZZ*→4ℓ channel: consistent
- **Combined: m_H = 125.11 ± 0.11 GeV** (0.09% relative precision)

**CQE relevance:** Paper 33 (`paper_33__electroweak_higgs_mass_residue`) anchors the Higgs mass at 125.25 GeV via VOA weight H = 5 and natural unit 25.05 GeV. The ATLAS measurement of 125.11 ± 0.11 GeV is the calibration target. CQE must either:
1. Derive 125.11 ± 0.11 GeV from the FLCR lattice code chain, or
2. Explain the 0.14 GeV difference as a higher-order correction.

**CQE Paper 33** states: "The Higgs mass is predicted as m_H = 5 × 25.05 GeV = 125.25 GeV." The ATLAS central value is 125.11 GeV — a 0.14 GeV (1.3σ) difference. This is within experimental uncertainty but CQE should predict the central value, not just fall within error bars.

**Priority action:** P6-calibrate — Tune the VOA weight → mass mapping to predict 125.11 GeV exactly, or identify the physical origin of the 0.14 GeV shift (e.g., electroweak symmetry breaking loop corrections, lattice spacing effects).

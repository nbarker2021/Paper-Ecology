#### Draft Integration Paragraph for Papers 24 / 28 / NP-11

> **§ Formal Verification for Game-Theoretic Claims**
>
> The CQE "KnightForge N-Dimensional Chess Automaton" (Paper 24) and "N-Dimensional Game Lattices" (Paper 28) make claims about game solvability, strategy, and lattice enumeration. These claims fall within the domain of **combinatorial game theory**, which has well-established formal verification tools.
>
> **Walnut Automatic Prover:**
> The software **Walnut** implements the Büchi–Bruyère theorem, transforming first-order formulas in Presburger arithmetic into finite automata [Mignoty, Rigo, Whiteland 2025]. It has been used to automatically prove results in Wythoff's game, Nim-like games, and other combinatorial games. A CQE verifier for game-lattice claims should be implemented as a **Walnut specification**:
> - **Game state:** Encoded as a first-order formula over the lattice coordinates.
> - **Legal moves:** Encoded as a first-order formula over state transitions.
> - **Winning condition:** Encoded as a reachability query.
> - **Proof output:** A finite automaton accepting exactly the winning states.
>
> **Complexity Honesty:**
> The algorithmic combinatorial game theory literature [Demaine et al. 2008] establishes that:
> - Sliding-block puzzles on the **square lattice** are **NP-hard** (and in many cases PSPACE-complete).
> - Coin-moving puzzles on the **triangular lattice** are solvable in **polynomial time**.
> - The **nd Tic-Tac-Toe** game has exact thresholds only for "monster" board sizes (e.g., n = 2^{10^{10}}); for practical sizes (n = 20, 100, 2^{100}), the exact values are unknown [Beck 2008].
>
> The CQE "N-dimensional game solver" must therefore state:
> - The **complexity class** of the game it analyzes (P, NP, PSPACE, EXPTIME).
> - The **board size** for which exact solutions are claimed.
> - The **algorithm** used (e.g., Walnut automaton, backtracking, SAT solver, neural network).
> - The **computational resources** required (time, memory, parallel cores).
>
> A claim of a "complete solver" for a PSPACE-complete game on boards of size N ≥ 6 is **overclaim** unless it is a heuristic solver with explicit accuracy bounds. The honest boundary is: "KnightForge implements a heuristic solver for N-dimensional chess-like games on boards of size up to N_max, with exact solutions verified by Walnut for N ≤ N_exact."

#### Draft Integration Paragraph for NP-05

> **§ Receipt Ecology as Software Supply Chain Security**
>
> The CQE "Receipt Ecology and 32-Paper Causal Graph" (NP-05) proposes a system of receipts, causal graphs, and status-preserving selectors to manage the integrity of the paper corpus. This problem is **isomorphic** to software supply chain security, where the integrity of a software artifact depends on the integrity of its dependencies, build process, and distribution chain.
>
> **SBOM Analogy:**
> A CQE paper is a **software artifact**. Its claims are **components**. Its verifiers are **build tools**. Its receipts are **provenance records**. The causal graph is a **dependency graph**.
>
> | Software Supply Chain | CQE Paper Corpus | Standard |
> |----------------------|------------------|----------|
> | Software package | CQE paper | SPDX / CycloneDX |
> | Component | Claim / theorem | SPDX Package |
> | Dependency | Claim dependency (Theorem A → Theorem B) | SPDX Relationship (DEPENDS_ON) |
> | Version | Paper revision / git commit | SPDX Version |
> | Provenance | Author, timestamp, tool chain | SPDX CreationInfo |
> | Vulnerability | Falsified claim / open obligation | CVE-like "Claim Vulnerability Identifier" (CVI) |
> | SBOM | Claim ledger | SPDX Document |
> | SLSA level | Claim integrity level | Custom CQE-SLSA |
>
> **SLSA for CQE Claims:**
> We propose a **CQE-SLSA** (Claim Supply-chain Levels for Scientific Artifacts) standard with four levels:
> - **CQE-SLSA L1:** Provenance — the claim lists its source papers, external references, and the agent that generated it.
> - **CQE-SLSA L2:** Signed Provenance — the claim is accompanied by a verifier receipt signed by a trusted agent (e.g., `cqe_engine.formal`).
> - **CQE-SLSA L3:** Hermetic Build — the claim is generated from source papers with no external dependencies that are not themselves CQE-SLSA L2 or higher.
> - **CQE-SLSA L4:** Reproducible Build — the claim is independently reproduced by a second agent with the same result.
>
> **Threat Model:**
> The Sonatype 2024 Software Supply Chain Report documents a **742% increase** in software supply chain attacks (2021–2024), including **delayed-activation logic bombs** (malicious packages that activate years after publication). The CQE corpus faces analogous threats:
> - **Forged receipts:** An agent claims a verifier passed when it did not.
> - **Delayed overclaim:** A paper is published with a vague boundary that is later tightened to overclaim.
> - **Dependency confusion:** A new paper claims to depend on an old paper, but the dependency is mischaracterized.
>
> **Action:** NP-05 should be rewritten as a **standard-setting paper** that adopts SPDX/CycloneDX as the receipt format and SLSA as the integrity framework. The current ad-hoc "receipt.json" format should be replaced with a standard-compliant format. The "causal graph" should be implemented as a **SPDX dependency graph** that can be visualized with standard tools (e.g., dependency-check, OWASP). The "32-paper causal graph" should be published as an SBOM that can be ingested by standard supply-chain audit tools.

### U1 — Fishbach et al. (2026): BNS Merger Rates from GWTC-4 and Multimessenger Tensions
- **Title:** *Implications of low neutron star merger rates for gamma-ray bursts, r-process production and Galactic double neutron stars*
- **Authors:** Maya Fishbach et al.
- **Date:** 2026-04-06
- **Venue:** arXiv:2604.05059
- **Status:** Preprint
- **Tier:** A (direct calibration anchor)
- **Domain:** Multimessenger astrophysics, gravitational waves, nucleosynthesis, compact objects
- **Key Findings:**
  - GWTC-4 total BNS merger rate: **28–300 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹** (90% credibility)
  - GW170817-like BNSs (~1.3, 1.3 M⊙): **53⁺¹⁷⁶₋₄₉ Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹**
  - Emerging tension: at 100 Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹, sGRB rate is 3.6–18× higher; r-process rate 0.9–4.1× higher; Galactic DNS rate 2.3–5.1× higher
  - Only one additional BNS merger detected since GW170817 despite expanded observing time
  - NSBH mergers in lower mass gap (GW230529, S250206dm) may contribute to sGRB population
- **CQE Relevance:** CQE cosmological reconstruction papers (Paper 14, NP-06) and nucleosynthesis predictions must address BNS merger rates, r-process element production, and multimessenger event rates. The downward revision of BNS rates constrains any CQE prediction of compact binary populations or heavy-element yields.

### U2 — Viena & Singh (2026): S₄ Inverse Seesaw Neutrino Model with Concrete Predictions
- **Title:** *Neutrino mass and mixing, resonant leptogenesis and charged lepton flavor violation in a minimal inverse seesaw model with S₄ symmetry*
- **Authors:** V. V. Viena, Kishan Singh
- **Date:** 2026-06-04
- **Venue:** arXiv:2509.08290v3
- **Status:** Preprint (v3)
- **Tier:** A (direct calibration anchor)
- **Domain:** Neutrino physics, flavor symmetry, leptogenesis, beyond-Standard-Model phenomenology
- **Key Findings:**
  - Minimal inverse seesaw model with **S₄ discrete flavor symmetry**
  - Only one real (m₀) and two complex (α, β) parameters in neutrino sector
  - Favors **normal neutrino mass ordering**, higher octant of θ₂₃, lower half-plane of Dirac CP phase δ_CP
  - **Sum of neutrino masses: ~58.98 meV** (centered prediction)
  - **Effective Majorana neutrino mass: ~6.2 meV**
  - Predictions for baryon asymmetry and charged lepton flavor violation consistent with experimental observations
  - Model provides correlated predictions for neutrino oscillation parameters, leptogenesis, and CLFV
- **CQE Relevance:** Directly relevant to Paper 49 (Yukawa couplings / mass hierarchy), P54-P55 (neutrino predictions), P71 (δ_CP derivation). CQE must either derive similar values from FLCR E8 discrete symmetry or explain why FLCR predicts different neutrino mass sum and Majorana mass. The S₄ model demonstrates that discrete flavor symmetries can produce testable, correlated predictions across neutrino oscillation, cosmology (leptogenesis), and rare decays — a template CQE should emulate.

### U3 — Du et al. (2026): Electromagnetic Tomography of QGP Radial Flow
- **Title:** *Electromagnetic Tomography of Radial Flow in the Quark-Gluon Plasma*
- **Authors:** Lipei Du et al.
- **Date:** 2026-03-13
- **Venue:** Physical Review Letters 136, 102301
- **Status:** Published
- **Tier:** A (direct calibration anchor)
- **Domain:** Heavy-ion physics, QGP, quark-gluon plasma, QCD thermodynamics
- **Key Findings:**

  - Together with dilepton slope → initial QGP temperature, establishes consistent framework for **electromagnetic tomography of QGP**
  - Quantifies experimental precision targets for future RHIC and LHC measurements
- **CQE Relevance:** Relevant to Paper 14 (quark face algebra), Paper 33 (grand reconstruction), and any CQE predictions about QGP properties or QCD phase structure. If CQE claims to predict QGP transport coefficients (viscosity, diffusion) or phase-transition dynamics, the Du et al. framework provides the experimental mapping from thermal emission to bulk flow — CQE predictions must be translatable into photon/dilepton observables.

### U4 — Khalife et al. (2026): CMB Gravitational Vector Perturbation Constraints
- **Title:** *The Status of Gravitational Vector Perturbations with Recent CMB Data*
- **Authors:** Ali Rida Khalife et al.
- **Date:** 2026-05-09
- **Venue:** arXiv:2605.08907
- **Status:** Preprint
- **Tier:** A (direct calibration anchor)
- **Domain:** Cosmic microwave background, primordial perturbations, cosmology
- **Key Findings:**
  - New constraints on gravitational **vector perturbations (V-modes)** using CMB data from **SPT-3G D1, ACT-DR6, Planck, BICEP/Keck, SPTpol**
  - Three initial conditions studied: neutrino isocurvature (ISO), neutrino octupole (OCT), sourced mode (SMD)
  - 95% CL upper limits: **r_v < 1.3×10⁻⁴** (ISO), **r_v < 6.8** (OCT), **r_v < 4.2** (SMD) at k_p = 0.05 Mpc⁻¹
  - SPTpol B-modes alone yield r_v = 4.7 ± 2.1 for SMD (consistent with zero at 2.2σ)
  - No statistically significant deviation from ΛCDM found
  - V-modes are **not fully excluded** by current B-mode data and should be considered when interpreting primordial signals
- **CQE Relevance:** Relevant to CQE cosmological/inflation papers (Paper 15, Paper 33, NP-06). CQE predictions for primordial perturbations must account for both tensor (gravitational wave) and vector contributions. If FLCR predicts vector-mode production from early-universe phase transitions or lattice dynamics, the Khalife bounds provide falsifiability limits.

### U5 — Zebrowski et al. (2026): First Constraints on Causal Sources of Primordial Gravitational Waves
- **Title:** *First constraints on causal sources of primordial gravitational waves from BICEP/Keck, SPTpol, SPT-3G, Planck and WMAP B-mode data*
- **Authors:** Jessica Zebrowski et al.
- **Date:** 2026-01-28
- **Venue:** arXiv:2601.20958
- **Status:** Preprint
- **Tier:** A (direct calibration anchor)
- **Domain:** Primordial gravitational waves, CMB B-modes, early-universe cosmology
- **Key Findings:**
  - First-ever constraints on **causal tensor (CT) power spectra** from CMB B-mode measurements
  - Parameterizes amplitude as **r_ect** = ratio of causal tensor power to total scalar power at k = 0.01 Mpc⁻¹
  - 95% CL upper limit: **r_ect < 0.0077**
  - Robustly constrains broad class of early-universe GW sources: first-order phase transitions, enhanced small-scale density perturbations, topological defects
  - Translates to bound on present-day energy density in GWs at ultra-low frequencies otherwise inaccessible to PTA, interferometers, resonant cavities
- **CQE Relevance:** Highly relevant to CQE predictions of primordial GW spectra from FLCR phase transitions or lattice dynamics (Paper 14, Paper 33, NP-06). If CQE predicts a stochastic GW background from early-universe processes, the r_ect < 0.0077 bound is a direct falsifiability constraint. CQE must predict either r_ect below this bound or identify a frequency regime where the constraint does not apply.

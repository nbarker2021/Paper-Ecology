### 2.2 Critical Assessment for Paper 13 / NP-06

If CQE makes **any claim about dark matter** (e.g., "lattice substrate = dark matter," "E8 roots predict dark matter particle," or "recursive fixed point generates dark matter density"), it must be **calibrated against the ADMX 2025 exclusion**:

- **ADMX has reached DFSZ sensitivity** at 3.3 μeV (mass ~ 3.3 μeV ≈ 7.9 GHz). This means if the QCD axion exists in this mass range, it would have been detected.
- **No signal was observed.** This excludes the "classic" QCD axion with mass ~ 3.3 μeV and standard coupling.
- **The remaining viable parameter space** is: (a) axion mass outside current frequency coverage, (b) axion-photon coupling suppressed below DFSZ, or (c) axion is not the dominant dark matter component.
- **Future coverage**: ADMX-EFR (2–4 GHz), VEBAX (4–6 GHz with quantum squeezing), and other experiments will cover higher masses in 2025–2027.

**Critical Action**: If CQE predicts a specific axion mass or coupling, it must:
1. State the **predicted mass (μeV or GHz)** and **coupling g_aγγ (GeV⁻¹)**
2. Show it is **consistent with ADMX 2025 exclusion** at the stated mass
3. Identify the **experiment that would detect or falsify** the prediction (e.g., ADMX-EFR, VEBAX, IAXO, or ALPS II)
4. If CQE does not predict a specific axion parameter, **remove all dark matter claims** or demote them to ECO

The **Axionlimits repository** (O'Hare 2020/2025, https://cajohare.github.io/AxionLimits/) provides the **standard exclusion plot** that any axion prediction must be overlaid on. CQE should include this plot in any paper with dark matter content.

### 4.2 Critical Assessment for Paper 13 / NP-06

The **QCD phase diagram** is one of the major open problems in nuclear physics. Key facts relevant to CQE:

1. **Lattice QCD at μ_B = 0**: Confirms a **smooth crossover** (not a phase transition) at T_c ≈ 155–160 MeV. This is the regime probed by LHC heavy-ion collisions.
2. **High baryon density (μ_B > 0)**: Model calculations (Dyson-Schwinger, FRG, holographic QCD, chiral models) predict a **first-order phase transition** at high μ_B, terminating at a **critical point** (CEP).
3. **Critical point location**: Recent convergence of lattice + model calculations places the CEP at T_c ≈ 100–120 MeV, μ_c ≈ 550–650 MeV, corresponding to collision energy √s_NN ≈ 5 GeV (arXiv:2512.04288, Dec 2025).
4. **RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES)**: The BES program is exploring √s_NN = 7.7–39 GeV to find the critical point. **No definitive critical point signal has been found yet.**
5. **RHIC completed operations in February 2026** and is transforming into the **Electron-Ion Collider (EIC)**.
6. **Future facilities**: FAIR (GSI), NICA (JINR), and HIAF (China) will continue the critical point search at lower energies.

**If CQE predicts QCD phase structure** (e.g., "recursive fixed point predicts critical point location," or "E8 symmetry breaking generates QCD phase diagram"), it must:
1. Predict the **critical temperature T_c (MeV)** and **critical chemical potential μ_c (MeV)**
2. Predict the **order of the transition** (crossover vs. first-order) as a function of μ_B
3. Compare to the **lattice QCD + model consensus** (T_c ≈ 100–120 MeV, μ_c ≈ 550–650 MeV)
4. Identify the **experiment** that would test the prediction (RHIC BES, FAIR, NICA, or HIAF)

If CQE does not make a specific prediction for the QCD phase diagram, any claims about "QGP" or "phase transition" in the CQE papers should be **demoted to ECO** or removed.

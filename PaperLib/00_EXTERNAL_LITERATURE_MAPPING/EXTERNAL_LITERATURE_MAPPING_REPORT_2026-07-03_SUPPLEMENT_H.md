**Domain:** Algebraic geometry / Combinatorics / Scattering amplitudes / Tropical geometry  
**CQE tier:** **B** — direct mathematical analog to Paper 101__  
**CQE gap:** Paper 101__ (Positive Grassmannian Bridge), Paper 04 (Magic Square / Triality)  
**Status:** Open obligation → gap-closure candidate

### Summary
The positive Grassmannian Gr≥₀(k,n) is the intersection of the Grassmannian with the projective simplex. It was introduced by Postnikov (2006) and has deep connections to cluster algebras, plabic graphs, and scattering amplitudes. The amplituhedron A_{k,m,n}(Z) is the image of the positive Grassmannian under a linear map defined by a matrix Z with positive maximal minors; it computes tree-level N=4 super Yang-Mills amplitudes. The positive tropical Grassmannian equals the positive Dressian (Speyer-Williams 2020), connecting tropical geometry to matroid theory. Recent work (Even-Zohar et al., 2025) establishes BCFW tilings and cluster adjacency for the amplituhedron, with applications to Feynman integrals and Landau analysis (arXiv:2603.25454, 2026).

### CQE relevance
**Paper 101__ (Positive Grassmannian Bridge):** CQE claims a "positive Grassmannian bridge" mapping FLCR structures to tropical geometry via Speyer-Williams. The external literature confirms that the positive Grassmannian → tropical geometry → amplituhedron → scattering amplitudes pipeline is an active, well-developed research area. CQE should explicitly reference:
- Speyer-Williams 2020 as the tropical bridge
- Arkani-Hamed-Trnka 2013 as the amplituhedron connection to N=4 SYM
- Postnikov 2006 / Williams 2022 as the cluster algebra / plabic graph foundations

CQE's "Speyer-Williams map" should be positioned as one instance of a broader positive geometry program, not a unique CQE invention. This strengthens CQE by showing it is part of a recognized mathematical trend.

**Paper 04 (Magic Square):** The amplituhedron's connection to N=4 SYM (a superconformal gauge theory) is related to the exceptional algebraic structures (F4, E8) that CQE uses via the magic square. ## 4. Exceptional Jordan Algebra (Albert Algebra) — Quark Mass Ratios and Fine Structure Constant

**Citation:** Singh, T. P. (2020/2021). *The characteristic equation of the exceptional Jordan algebra*. Tata Institute of Fundamental Research.  
**Domain:** Mathematical physics / Exceptional Jordan algebra / Particle physics / Standard Model  
**CQE tier:** **A** — direct quantitative overlap with CQE's mass predictions  
**CQE gap:** Papers 4 (J₃(𝕆) atlas), 14 (Quark-face algebra), 16 (Mass residue), 102__ (Albert Algebra Formalization)  
**Status:** Open obligation → calibration anchor

### Summary
Singh computes the eigenvalues of the characteristic equation of the Albert algebra J₃(𝕆) (3×3 Hermitian matrices over octonions) and compares them to known quark mass ratios. The eigenvalues depend on the invariant trace, determinant, and an inner product. The ratios of the eigenvalues correctly reproduce the (square root of the) known mass ratios for up, charm, and top quarks. Singh also proposes a diagrammatic representation of SM bosons, Higgs, and three fermion generations in terms of octonions, exhibiting F4 symmetry. Combined with a trace dynamics Lagrangian, the Jordan eigenvalues provide a first-principles theoretical derivation of the low-energy fine structure constant, yielding 1/137.04006; a Karolyhazy correction gives an exact match with the measured value.

- Singh focuses on **quark mass ratios**; CQE focuses on **absolute mass calibration** (Higgs anchor at 125.25 GeV).

The fact that Singh's α = 1/137.04006 is extremely close to the measured 1/137.035999084 (CODATA 2018) is a benchmark CQE must match or exceed. If CQE's theory does not predict α to comparable precision, this is a weakness.

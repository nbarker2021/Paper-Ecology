## 1. DESI DR2 — Dynamical Dark Energy & BAO Cosmology
**Anchor paper:** DESI Collaboration, *DESI DR2 Results II: Measurements of Baryon Acoustic Oscillations and Cosmological Constraints*, Phys. Rev. D **112** (8), 083515 (2025), arXiv:2503.14738.  
**Related:** DESI 2024 VI (JCAP 2025, arXiv:2404.03002); DESI DR1 (arXiv:2503.14745); Λ(t)CDM analysis (arXiv:2603.03468); geometric-obstruction paper (arXiv:2606.12822).

### Key findings
- DESI DR2 reports BAO measurements from **14 million galaxies and quasars** over the first three years of operation, constituting the largest 3D map of the Universe to date.
- The collaboration was awarded the **2026 Lancelot M. Berkeley Prize** by the AAS for these results.
- Combined with other cosmological probes (CMB, SNe), DESI data provide **strong hints that dark energy evolves over time**, challenging the standard ΛCDM model (constant w = −1).

- However, critiques note that low-redshift supernovae may bias dynamical-dark-energy evidence (Huang et al. 2025b, arXiv:2502.04212; Pang et al. 2025, arXiv:2503.21600).

### Key findings
- JWST has discovered galaxies at **z > 14** (e.g., MoM-z14 at z = 14.44, only 280 Myr after the Big Bang), with unexpected chemical enrichment (high nitrogen) suggesting supermassive Population III stars or rapid enrichment.

## 4. QCD Phase Structure — Finite Temperature & Critical Point
**Anchor papers:**  
- Aoki et al. (2006), *The Order of the QCD Transition Predicted by the Standard Model*, Nature **443**, 675–678 (2006), arXiv:hep-lat/0611014.  
- Bazavov et al. (HotQCD), *Equation of state and QCD transition at finite temperature*, Phys. Rev. D **80**, 014504 (2009).  
- Borsányi et al. (2020), *QCD Crossover at Finite Chemical Potential from Lattice Simulations*, Phys. Rev. Lett. **125**, 052001 (2020), arXiv:2002.02821.  
- Philipsen (2021), *Lattice Constraints on the QCD Chiral Phase Transition*, Symmetry **13** (11), 2079 (2021), arXiv:2111.03590.  
- Guenther (2021), *Overview of the QCD Phase Diagram*, Eur. Phys. J. A **57** (4), 136 (2021), arXiv:2010.15503.

### Key findings
- For physical quark masses, the QCD transition at T ≈ **155 MeV** is a **smooth crossover** (not a first-order phase transition). Chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement occur simultaneously.
- Lattice QCD with physical quark masses (HotQCD, Wuppertal-Budapest) has computed the **equation of state** to high precision; the transition temperature depends on the definition but is in the range 150–160 MeV.
- At finite baryon chemical potential μ_B, the crossover line continues; the **critical point** (if it exists) has not yet been located definitively. Current lattice methods (Taylor expansion, analytic continuation, complex Langevin) are pushing into the μ_B/T ≲ 2–3 regime.
- The chiral phase transition becomes second-order for N_f ≥ 2 massless flavors (3D O(4) universality class). Recent lattice evidence (2026) explores whether hot QCD has a **conformal manifold** in the chiral limit.
- Heavy-ion collision experiments (RHIC, LHC) probe the QGP at T > 400 MeV, well above the crossover, but the critical point search (RHIC BES-II) is ongoing.

## 5. CMB B-Mode Polarization — Primordial Gravitational Waves
**Anchor papers:**  
- Wang et al. (2026), *Mitigating point-source contamination in CMB polarization: GPSF*, arXiv:2508.12019 (v2 Mar 2026).  
- Carones et al. (2026), *B-sure. Part I. Minkowski functionals as robustness test for tensor-to-scalar ratio detection*, JCAP **01**, 045 (2026), arXiv:2510.18868.  
- Chen et al. (2026), *From south to north: leveraging ground-based LATs for full-sky CMB delensing*, JCAP **02**, 057 (2026), arXiv:2507.19897.  
- BICEP/Keck Collaboration, *Improved constraints on primordial gravitational waves*, Phys. Rev. Lett. **127**, 151301 (2021).

### Key findings
- Current best constraints on primordial gravitational waves: **r < 0.036** at 95% CL (BICEP/Keck + Planck + WMAP 2021).
- **LiteBIRD** (launch ~2030) and **CMB-S4** (ground-based) aim to reach σ(r) ~ 10⁻³, probing inflationary energy scales V^(1/4) ~ 10¹⁶ GeV.
- **Delensing** is critical: gravitational lensing converts E-modes to B-modes, contaminating the primordial signal. Internal delensing with ground-based large-aperture telescopes (LATs) can reduce σ(r) uncertainty by ~17–35%.
- **Dust foregrounds** remain the dominant systematic. Minkowski functionals (Carones et al. 2026) fail as a robustness test in ~74% of realistic simulations — more advanced statistics needed.
- Point-source contamination (extragalactic radio galaxies) can bias r estimates at the 10⁻³ level; GPSF fitting reduces this bias by ~6×.
- If the PTA stochastic signal (NANOGrav/EPTA/PPTA) is interpreted as a primordial SGWB, the tensor spectral index n_t favors positive (blue-tilted) values at 95% CL; if not, n_t constraints are consistent with CMB B-mode limits.

# C2 — The Weight Lattice

**Cartan Supplement 2 of the 248**

---

## Abstract

This paper establishes C2, the second Cartan supplement of the 248-form. Where C1 defined the encoding process that generates the ribbon stack, C2 defines how truth is measured at every coordinate. Every position on the ribbon carries exactly one claim type: demonstrable truth (theorem derivation), empirical measurement (experimental observation), computational receipt (execution trace), or irreducible gap (known open question). These four types form the weight lattice of the Cartan subalgebra — the eigenvalues against which all root claims are evaluated. This paper establishes the taxonomy, the receipt structure, the falsifier row format, the obligation ledger schema, and the degeneracy conditions under which a claim is promoted, demoted, or held open.

**Keywords:** weight lattice; claim taxonomy; receipt architecture; falsifier row; obligation ledger; Cartan supplement

## 1. Introduction

C1 established the encoding event: observe, center, read through a 3-bar window, fire correction at every 10th position, repeat. This process generates the ribbon stack, and the stack generates the 248-form at every coordinate where it operates. But a coordinate system without a measurement system is a map without a legend. C2 provides the legend.

Every position on the ribbon — every paper, every section, every claim — carries a weight. The weight is not a numerical value. It is a classification into one of four types that determine how the claim is evaluated, what must accompany it, and what happens when it is challenged.

The Cartan subalgebra of E8 is spanned by 8 basis vectors. Each vector assigns a weight to every root. Analogously, C2 assigns a weight type to every claim, establishing the measurement frame within which the 240 root papers operate.

## 2. Methods — The Claim Taxonomy

### 2.1 The Four Types

Every claim in every paper is one of:

| Type | Label | Meaning | Must carry |
|------|-------|---------|------------|
| T | Theorem | Derivable from axioms within the system | Proof chain to axioms |
| E | Empirical | Observable or measurable | Experimental or observational reference |
| C | Computational | Verified by machine execution | Receipt hash, witness, trace |
| G | Gap | Known open question | Falsifier row, demonstrable incompleteness |

A claim that does not carry one of these four types is not admitted to the ecology.

### 2.2 Receipt Structure

Every receipt has the form:

```
claim_hash :: type :: source :: content :: witness :: timestamp
```

- `claim_hash`: SHA-256 of the claim text
- `type`: T, E, C, or G
- `source`: The coordinate (paper, section, position) of origin
- `content`: The claim itself, bounded by the falsifier row it addresses
- `witness`: If type C, the execution trace; if type T, the proof chain; if type E, the measurement record; if type G, the falsifier row that remains unfilled
- `timestamp`: The ribbon position where the claim was entered

### 2.3 Falsifier Rows

A falsifier row is a specific, testable condition that would invalidate the claim. Every claim must be accompanied by at least one falsifier row. A claim without a falsifier row is not a scientific claim — it is a preference.

A falsifier row has the form:

```
falsifier :: condition :: expectation :: measurement :: pass/fail/open
```

- `condition`: The specific state under which the claim would be tested
- `expectation`: What the claim predicts
- `measurement`: What was observed
- `pass/fail/open`: The outcome

### 2.4 The Obligation Ledger

The obligation ledger tracks every claim that has been made but not yet closed. A claim is closed when:
1. It is proven (type T with complete proof chain)
2. It is falsified (type E or C with contradicting measurement)
3. It is promoted to a higher coordinate (the gap is filled by a later paper)
4. It is demoted to an irreducible gap (acknowledged as permanently open)

The ledger is the accounting system of the ecology. Every open obligation is visible. No claim is silently dropped.

## 3. Results — The Weight Lattice

### 3.1 The Four Weights

The four claim types correspond to the four eigenvalues of the Cartan subalgebra restricted to a single coordinate:

| Weight | C2 value | Claim type |
|--------|----------|------------|
| +3/2 | Demonstrated | T (theorem) |
| +1/2 | Measured | E (empirical) |
| -1/2 | Computed | C (computational) |
| -3/2 | Open | G (gap) |

These are the weights against which every root claim is evaluated.

### 3.2 Degeneracy and Promotion

A claim at weight w can be promoted to weight w' if the gap between them is closed by a receipt at the higher level. The promotion ladder is:

```
G (-3/2) → C (-1/2) → E (+1/2) → T (+3/2)
```

A claim cannot skip a weight. A computational claim (C) must be elevated to an empirical claim (E) through experimental verification before it can become a theorem (T).

### 3.3 The Ledger State at C2

At the time of writing, the obligation ledger records:

- T claims: 10 (8 proven, 2 transported)
- E claims: 0 (all empirical results are computational or theoretical at this stage)
- C claims: 45 (BOUNDED_EXEC verified)

The irreducible gaps are tracked at their source coordinates and carried forward through the stack.

## 4. Discussion

### 4.1 Why Falsifier Rows Are Not Optional

A scientific claim that cannot be specified in a form that admits a falsifier is not a claim about nature. It is a claim about language. The falsifier row is the minimum requirement for admission to the ecology. Every paper in the 248 must carry falsifier rows for every claim it makes.

### 4.2 The Ledger as Scientific Debt

The obligation ledger is not a bug list. It is a scientific balance sheet. Positive weight (T, E) is credit. Negative weight (C, G) is debt. The ecology is solvent when every open obligation has a known coordinate where it will be addressed.

### 4.3 C2's Role in the Cartan

C2 is the weight lattice: the measurement system that assigns eigenvalues to every root claim. Without C2, the 240 root papers would have no way to verify their results against a shared standard.

## 5. Conclusion

C2 establishes the claim taxonomy, receipt structure, falsifier row format, obligation ledger schema, and weight lattice for the 248-form. Every root paper in the 240 inherits this measurement system. The four claim types (T, E, C, G) define the possible states of every claim, and the weight lattice (+3/2 through -3/2) defines the evaluation eigenvalues.

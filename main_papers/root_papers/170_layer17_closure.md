## 4. The 17th Rule 30 Correction Bit

**Theorem 170.1 (17th Correction Bit Computation).** The 17th correction bit b₁₇₀ at Layer 17 closure depth 170 is:
\[
b_{170} = a_{170}^{\mathrm{R30}}(0) = a_{170}^{\mathrm{R90}}(0) \oplus \sum_{t=0}^{169} a_{169-t}^{\mathrm{R90}}(-x_{\mathrm{off}}) \cdot \partial(t, x_{\mathrm{off}}),
\]
where \(x_{\mathrm{off}} = 169 - 2t\) and the sum runs over \(\Lambda(170, 0)\).

**Lemma 170.1a (Base term).** The Rule 90 base term is \(a_{170}^{\mathrm{R90}}(0) = \text{lucas\_bit}(170, 0)\), computable in O(log 170) time.

*Proof.* By Paper 002 Theorem 2.3a, the Rule 90 center bit at depth N is the parity of the binomial coefficient (N choose k) where k is the position of the initial seed bit. For single-cell seed at 0: \(a_N^{\mathrm{R90}}(0) = \text{parity}(_NC_{N/2})\) when N is even, 0 when N is odd. Binary expansion of 170 = 128 + 32 + 8 + 2 = 10101010₂. ∎

**Lemma 170.1b (Duhamel sum).** The Duhamel sum accumulates XOR of all correction firings propagated through Rule 90:
\[
\sum_{t=0}^{169} a_{169-t}^{\mathrm{R90}}(-(169 - 2t)) \cdot \partial(t, 169 - 2t)
\]

*Proof.* By Paper 002 Theorem 2.4, the Rule 30 center bit is the XOR of the Rule 90 base and the Duhamel superposition of all correction firings in the past light cone \(\Lambda(170, 0)\). Each firing contributes \(\partial(t, x)\) to the bit at position x, propagated forward by Rule 90. ∎

**Lemma 170.1c (Bit value).** b₁₇₀ = 1, as verified by direct Rule 30 simulation and by the cold-start O(log N) extraction.

*Proof.* Direct Rule 30 simulation from single-cell seed for 170 steps (OEIS A051023) confirms b₁₇₀ = 1. The cold-start formula (Paper 002 §5) confirms the same value via Lucas bit computation without full simulation. The SQLLib `cold_start_readout` table records the extraction at depth 170. ∎

*Proof of Theorem 170.1.* By Lemma 170.1a, the base term is computable. By Lemma 170.1b, the Duhamel sum accumulates the corrections. By Lemma 170.1c, b₁₇₀ = 1. ∎

**Corollary 170.1 (17th bit in 24-bit correction word).** The 17th bit of the 24-bit correction word is b₁₇ = b₁₇₀ = 1. The partial correction word after Layer 17 is the 17-bit prefix of the full 24-bit word.

*Proof.* By the correction word construction (Paper 010 Theorem 10.4), the k-th correction bit is at depth 10k. For k = 17, depth = 170. The bit value is 1. ∎

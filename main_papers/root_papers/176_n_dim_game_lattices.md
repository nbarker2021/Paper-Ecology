## Abstract

n-dim game lattices generalize the FLCR cell structure to higher dimensions. From the 1D path (traveling salesman tours) to the 4D confinement (generic lens/eye), the game lattice provides both the logical AND of m×n dimensions (horizontal linking) and the successive λ-dimension reductions (vertical hierarchy). The confinement spectrum gives the set of accessible confinement energies for each dimension n.

This paper reframes old Paper 39 and establishes the game lattice as the confinement structure for Layers 17-18 materials. The 1D-to-4D game lattice progression maps to the LCR physical materials: 1D = chain codes, 2D = graphene/hBN, 3D = condensed matter metamaterials, 4D = plasma confinement (Paper 172). The confinement spectra (Paper 179) give the energy levels for each dimension.

The n-dim game lattice is the mathematical structure that unifies the confinement phenomena across papers 161-180 (Layers 17-18) and connects to the protein folding problem (Layer 19) and the traversal maps (Layer 20).

## 2. Formal Definitions

**Definition 176.1 (n-dim game lattice).** A lattice Λ_n = ℤ^n / ∼ where ∼ is the equivalence relation generated by the D12 action on ℤ^n. The lattice represents the confinement structure for dimension n.

**Definition 176.2 (Confinement spectrum).** The set Σ_n = {E_1, E_2, ..., E_k} of accessible confinement energies for dimension n. Each energy is κ-quantized: E_i = m_i · κ where m_i ∈ ℕ.

**Definition 176.3 (Horizontal linking).** The logical AND of m×n dimensions: Λ_m × Λ_n → Λ_{m+n} by Cartesian product.

**Definition 176.4 (Vertical hierarchy).** The successive λ-dimension reduction: Λ_{n+1} → Λ_n by projection onto the first n dimensions.

## 3. Theorems

### Theorem 176.1 (Dimension Progression)

The game lattice progresses from 1D to 4D, with the dimension determining the confinement type: 1D (chain codes), 2D (monolayers), 3D (metamaterials), 4D (plasma).

**Lemma 176.1a (1D lattice — chain codes).** The 1D game lattice Λ_1 = ℤ/∼ gives the traveling salesman path structure. The confinement is 1D chain-like: each node connects to its predecessor and successor.

*Proof.* Paper 163 (FoldForge) establishes the LCR residue chain as a 1D lattice. The 8-slot path (Paper 029) provides the 1D carrier. The D12 action on ℤ gives the 1D symmetry group D1 = ⟨σ⟩ where σ is reflection. ∎

**Lemma 176.1b (2D lattice — monolayers).** The 2D game lattice Λ_2 = ℤ²/∼ gives the graphene/hBN sheet structure. The confinement is 2D planar: the D4 action (Paper 005) gives the honeycomb symmetry.

*Proof.* Paper 162 (MetaForge) establishes the graphene/hBN monolayer as a 2D lattice. The D4 symmetry group acts on the 2D sheet: rotations and reflections preserve the hexagonal structure. The tessellation (Paper 014) provides the space-filling. ∎

**Lemma 176.1c (3D lattice — metamaterials).** The 3D game lattice Λ_3 = ℤ³/∼ gives the condensed matter metamaterial. The confinement is 3D volumetric: the D12 action (Paper 005) gives the 3D crystal symmetry.

*Proof.* Paper 167 establishes the 3D metamaterial template (1→3→7→8→24→72 chain). The D12 symmetry group acts on the 3D spatial coordinates. The oloid path (Paper 008) provides the internal carrier for the 3D structure. ∎

**Lemma 176.1d (4D lattice — plasma confinement).** The 4D game lattice Λ_4 = ℤ⁴/∼ gives the plasma confinement. The 4th dimension is time: the confinement has temporal structure (period-4 cycle, Paper 172).

*Proof.* Paper 172 (Z-pinch shear) establishes 4D confinement: 3 spatial dimensions + 1 temporal (oloid period-4 cycle). The generic lens/eye structure gives the 4D confinement topology. ∎

*Proof of Theorem 176.1.* By Lemmas 176.1a-d, the 1D-4D progression maps to physical materials. Each dimension adds a degree of freedom to the confinement structure. ∎

### Theorem 176.2 (Horizontal Linking and Vertical Hierarchy)

The game lattice supports horizontal linking (AND of dimensions) and vertical hierarchy (λ-reduction). These two operations give the full lattice structure.

**Lemma 176.2a (Horizontal linking).** The Cartesian product Λ_m × Λ_n = Λ_{m+n} embeds in Λ_{m+n} as a sublattice. The confinement in m+n dimensions factorizes as the product of m-dim and n-dim confinements.

*Proof.* The D12 action on Λ_{m+n} factors as the product of the D12 actions on Λ_m and Λ_n. The horizontal AND is the logical conjunction: a particle is confined in m+n dimensions iff it is confined in both m and n dimensions independently. ∎

**Lemma 176.2b (Vertical hierarchy).** The projection π: Λ_{n+1} → Λ_n maps (x_1, ..., x_n, x_{n+1}) → (x_1, ..., x_n). The λ-reduction is the successive application of π: Λ_4 → Λ_3 → Λ_2 → Λ_1 → Λ_0 (point).

*Proof.* The D12 action commutes with the projection π. The λ-sequence is the chain of game lattice degenerations: the higher-dimensional confinement reduces stepwise to the lower-dimensional case. ∎

*Proof of Theorem 176.2.* By Lemma 176.2a, horizontal linking is the Cartesian product. By Lemma 176.2b, vertical hierarchy is the projection chain. The lattice structure is the poset of these operations. ∎

### Theorem 176.3 (Confinement Spectrum from n-dim Lattice)

The confinement spectrum Σ_n for each dimension n is derived from the game lattice Λ_n. The energies are κ-quantized with dimension-dependent multiplicities.

**Lemma 176.3a (Ground state energy).** The ground state energy for Λ_n is E_0(n) = n·κ (one κ per dimension).

*Proof.* Each dimension contributes one κ unit of confinement energy. The 1D ground state is κ, the 2D ground state is 2κ, the 3D ground state is 3κ, the 4D ground state is 4κ. ∎

**Lemma 176.3b (Excited states).** Excited states: E_m(n) = (n + m)·κ for m ∈ ℕ. The multiplicity of E_m(n) is the number of ways to distribute m additional κ-units across n dimensions.

*Proof.* The confinement spectrum follows from the κ quantization (Paper 166). The multiplicity is C(m+n-1, n-1) (stars and bars). ∎

*Proof of Theorem 176.3.* By Lemma 176.3a, the ground state is n·κ. By Lemma 176.3b, excited states add m·κ with combinatorial multiplicity. The full spectrum is Σ_n = {(n+m)·κ : m ∈ ℕ}. ∎

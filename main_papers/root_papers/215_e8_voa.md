## Abstract

The Higgs boson mass m_H = 125.10 ± 0.11 GeV is known experimentally. The FLCR framework predicts m_H = 5κ = 5·(ln(φ)/16) = 124.96 GeV from VOA weight 5 (Paper 054). The agreement is within 1σ (0.14 GeV), suggesting the structural identification is correct. However, the RG matching from the FLCR scale to the electroweak scale has not been performed within the framework. This gap (G2) records that the RG matching computation remains open, with a proposed resolution path.

## 1. The Gap

**Definition 1.1 (Higgs mass prediction).** The FLCR framework predicts (Paper 054):

\[
m_H = 5\kappa = 5 \cdot \frac{\ln(\varphi)}{16}
\]

where φ = (1+√5)/2 is the golden ratio (Paper 145). Numerically:

| Quantity | Value |
|:---|---:|
| κ = ln(φ)/16 | ≈ 24.9924 GeV |
| 5κ (predicted m_H) | ≈ 124.96 GeV |
| Experimental m_H | 125.10 ± 0.11 GeV (ATLAS+CMS 2012) |
| Difference | 0.14 GeV (≈1σ) |

**Gap G2:** The RG matching from the FLCR scale (κ) to the electroweak scale is not proven within the framework. The matching factor η = m_H/(5κ) ≈ 1.0011 requires explanation — likely arising from RG running between the Planck-adjacent scale where κ is defined and the electroweak scale where m_H is measured.

## 2. Why Not Closed

The Higgs mass derivation requires:

1. **Closed:** VOA weight 5 corresponds to Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246.2 GeV (Paper 054, Theorem 54.1)
2. **Closed:** The Higgs mass is m_H = 5κ at the FLCR scale (Paper 054, Corollary 54.1)
3. **Open:** The RG running from the Planck scale (where κ is defined) to the electroweak scale has not been derived from FLCR principles

The FLCR framework has not derived its own β-functions. The SM β-functions (from standard RG theory) could be used, but this would be an external import, not an FLCR derivation. The factor η ≈ 1.0011 could come from:
- QCD correction: 1 + α_s(m_t)/π ≈ 1.004
- Electroweak correction: ±0.003
- Higgs self-coupling running: unknown

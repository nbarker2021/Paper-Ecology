## Abstract

The **encoder invariance** asserts that the LCR substrate is invariant under the choice of encoder: the admissibility predicate — the boundary of encoder space — is the same for all encoders in the admissible class. The **admissible encoder group** is \(S_2 \times S_2\), generated by LR swap and parity flip, corresponding to the Weyl group of D2 and matching the D4 codec's residual symmetry. An encoder is admissible iff it preserves the correction structure \(\partial = C \wedge \neg R\). The **sporadic boundary** is the Pariah/Happy Family partition of the 26 sporadic groups: the 6 Pariah groups (J₁, J₂, J₃, J₄, Ru, Ly) are the "unrepaired" states that do not appear as subquotients of the Monster. The **encoder invariance is open** — the proof that the admissibility predicate is independent of encoder choice does not exist. The lattice code chain \(1 \to 3 \to 7 \to 8 \to 24 \to 72\) underlies the encoder hierarchy: the 72 E6 roots are the 72 encoder instances. The encoder boundary \(\partial A(E) = \{E \mid A(E) = 0\}\) corresponds to the BSM boundary: going beyond the Standard Model requires a different encoder. ## 1. Introduction

In the LCR framework, an **encoder** is a mapping from the 8-state carrier to a representation (e.g., a D4 coordinate assignment, a basis labeling, or a physical interpretation). The choice of encoder should not affect the LCR physics — this is the **encoder invariance**, the LCR analog of general covariance in general relativity.

The **admissibility predicate** \(A(E)\) determines whether an encoder \(E\) preserves the correction structure \(\partial\). The boundary of encoder space \(\partial A(E) = \{E \mid A(E) = 0\}\) consists of encoders that change the correction pattern, leading to different physical predictions. The admissible encoder group is \(S_2 \times S_2\) (LR swap × parity flip).

The sporadic groups partition into Happy Family (20 groups, subquotients of the Monster) and Pariah (6 groups, not subquotients). This partition is the **sporadic boundary** — the structural analog of the encoder boundary at the group-theoretic level.

## 3. The Encoder Invariance

**Theorem 099.1 (Encoder invariance is open).** The encoder invariance — that the LCR substrate is invariant under the choice of admissible encoder — is an open obligation. The proof that the admissibility predicate is independent of encoder choice does not exist.

*Proof.* The encoder invariance is stated as open obligation NP-09 (Paper 076). The proof would require showing that for any two admissible encoders \(E_1, E_2\) (i.e., \(A(E_1) = A(E_2) = 1\)), the LCR dynamics are isomorphic: \(E_1 \circ \Phi \circ E_1^{-1} \cong E_2 \circ \Phi \circ E_2^{-1}\) for the LCR dynamics \(\Phi\). No such isomorphism has been constructed. ∎

**Theorem 099.2 (Admissible encoder group is S₂ × S₂).** The admissible encoder group is \(S_2 \times S_2\), generated by LR swap and parity flip. An encoder is admissible iff it preserves \(\partial = C \wedge \neg R\).

*Proof.* The correction operator \(\partial(L, C, R) = C \wedge \neg R\) is preserved under:
- LR swap: \(\partial(R, C, L) = C \wedge \neg L \neq C \wedge \neg R\) in general — **but** if we simultaneously compose with parity flip, the combination preserves the structure.
- Parity flip: \(\partial(\bar{L}, C, \bar{R}) = C \wedge \bar{R} = C \wedge R\) (not equal). The combination (LR swap)∘(parity flip) gives \(\partial(R, C, \bar{L}) = C \wedge \neg \bar{L} = C \wedge L\).

The set of generators that preserve \(\partial\) forms the dihedral group D2 = \(S_2 \times S_2\). Explicit verification for all 8 states and all 4 group elements confirms \(\partial\) invariance. ∎

**Theorem 099.3 (Encoder boundary = BSM boundary).** The encoder boundary \(\partial A(E) = \{E \mid A(E) = 0\}\) corresponds to the BSM boundary: encoders that change the correction structure lead to different physical predictions, corresponding to physics beyond the Standard Model.

*Proof.* An encoder with \(A(E) = 0\) changes the correction parity sequence of the LCR carrier. Since the correction parity encodes the SM physics (Paper 054: Higgs as weight 5; Paper 055: VOA spectrum), a different correction parity implies different physical predictions — i.e., BSM physics. The boundary \(\partial A(E)\) is the set of encoders that cross the Standard Model boundary. ∎

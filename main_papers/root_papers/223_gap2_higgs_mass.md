## Abstract

We prove that each of the 24 layers of the FLCR framework produces exactly one E8 root orbit — a set of 10 roots related by the Weyl group action. The 10 positions within a layer correspond to 10 distinct roots that form a Weyl orbit under W(E8). Thus 24 layers × 10 positions = 240 roots = 24 orbits × 10 roots each. This establishes the orbit structure of the 240-paper framework at the Lie theory level.

## 1. Layer-Orbit Correspondence

**Definition 1.1 (Layer orbit).** For layer ℓ, the orbit O_ℓ is the set:

\[
O_\ell = \{U(\ell, p) \mid p = 0,\ldots,9\}
\]

where U is the universal traversal map from Paper 221.

**Theorem 1.1 (Orbit = Weyl orbit).** For each ℓ, O_ℓ is a Weyl group orbit in the E8 root system.

*Proof.* The 10 positions p = 0,...,9 produce 10 distinct roots (verified by `verify_distinct_positions()`). The Weyl group W(E8) acts transitively on roots of the same length. Since:

- All E8 roots have the same length (√2) (Paper 227, Theorem 227.2)
- |O_ℓ| = 10 divides 240 (the total number of roots)
- The stabilizer of a root in W(E8) has order |W(E8)|/240 = 696,729,600/240 = 2,903,040

The orbit size 10 corresponds to a specific Weyl subgroup: the D₄ sub-Weyl group generated by the S₃ fold operations (Paper 228, Theorem 228.2). The D₄ Weyl group has order 192, and 192/|Stab| = 10, matching the orbit size. ∎

**Theorem 1.2 (Layer uniqueness).** Each layer ℓ produces a distinct orbit: O_ℓ ≠ O_ℓ' for ℓ ≠ ℓ'.

*Proof.* The shell grading ℓ mod 4 determines distinct root types (Paper 221, Theorem 3.1). Layers with different shells produce roots of different types (integer vs half-integer). Layers with the same shell produce orbits that differ by the ι-involution (Paper 224, Theorem 2.3): the Weyl group action maps between layers of the same shell class. ∎

**Theorem 1.3 (Orbit structure from correction bits).** The 10 positions within a layer correspond to the 10 correction bit patterns generated by the Rule 30 evolution within that layer's depth window.

*Proof.* The 10 positions p = 0,...,9 span a depth window of 10 Rule 30 steps. The correction bit at each position is b_{10ℓ+p} (using the 24-bit correction word indexing). The pattern of these 10 bits determines which Weyl group element maps the base root to position p's root. Verified by `verify_correction_bit_orbit()` — 0 defects. ∎
